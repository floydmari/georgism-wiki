## Start Here

This is a definitive, honestly sourced reference on **Geoism** — the idea that economic rents, beginning with the value of land and location, are created by the community and can be captured for public good. Land and the land-value tax are its core and best-evidenced case; the full scope reaches every kind of rent and every instrument for capturing it.

Whatever brought you here — building an argument, designing a policy, or checking a claim — the paths below get you to the strongest available evidence and the strongest counterargument, all cited.

## If you have 5 minutes

The five pages that carry the most weight:

1. [Land Value Tax](/wiki/land-value-tax/) — the core policy: what it is, why economists across the spectrum take it seriously, and who really bears it.
2. [Economic Rent](/wiki/economic-rent/) — the concept everything else rests on: value that accrues to a fixed factor rather than to effort or investment.
3. [Georgism](/wiki/georgism/) — the political-economic philosophy tying it together, named for Henry George.
4. [Evidence Dashboard](/wiki/evidence-dashboard/) — the graded state of the evidence at a glance: what is Strong, what is Contested, and where the gaps are.
5. [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/) — the person and the 1879 argument that started the movement.

- **To make the case** → the [Advocate's Arsenal](/wiki/advocates-arsenal/) for quotable, graded ammunition, and the [Evidence Dashboard](/wiki/evidence-dashboard/) for the state of the proof.

Eight portals, each a curated way into a cluster of the wiki:

- [Tax Design](/wiki/portal-tax-design/) — incidence, neutrality, assessment, and how a land tax is actually built.
- [Climate and the Commons](/wiki/portal-climate-and-commons/) — carbon pricing, resource royalties, and rent from the atmosphere and ecology.
- [History and People](/wiki/portal-history-and-people/) — George, the single-tax movement, and the thinkers before and after.
- [Case Studies](/wiki/portal-case-studies/) — real jurisdictions, from Pennsylvania split-rate towns to Norway's oil fund.
