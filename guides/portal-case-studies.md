## The real record

Theory says taxing land is efficient and shifting tax off buildings encourages
construction. The places below let you check that against what actually happened — and the
honest verdict is that reality is messier and more conditional than the clean model. Three
patterns recur. First, **split-rate and site-value taxation work in the predicted
direction but modestly**: Pittsburgh, the Pennsylvania towns, and now a Finnish natural
experiment show more construction where tax sits on land rather than buildings, but a New
Zealand null and self-selected data keep the finding short of "proven." Second, **resource
rent capture is the strongest story, but it is conditional on institutions**: Norway's
sovereign fund is the flagship, Alaska pays a real dividend, Botswana captured its diamond
rent — yet the resource curse is equally real where fiscal design and institutions are
weak. Third, and most important for advocates to internalize, **capturing land rent is not
the same as making housing cheap**: Singapore and Hong Kong fund their states from land to
a degree Georgists dream of, and both have punishing housing costs, because capture
without abundant supply raises revenue, not affordability.

The two Danish studies are carried here **in explicit tension** — one finds land taxes
capitalize into lower prices as theory predicts, the other reads the same terrain more
skeptically — because a definitive reference shows its disagreements rather than cherry-
picking the convenient one. The developing-world entries are the newest and, in RCT terms,
the most rigorous evidence in the whole file.

## Split-rate and site-value taxation

- [Pittsburgh](/wiki/pittsburgh/) — the longest-running split-rate experiment, and its 1979–80 building surge.
- [Oates & Schwab on Pittsburgh](/wiki/oates-schwab-pittsburgh/) — the study that isolated the construction effect from the regional boom.
- [Pennsylvania](/wiki/pennsylvania/) — the wider two-rate record across the state's cities.
- [Bourassa on Pittsburgh housing](/wiki/bourassa-pittsburgh-lvt-housing/) — the tempered read on housing effects.
- [Estonia](/wiki/estonia/) and [Tomson on the Estonian LVT](/wiki/tomson-estonia-lvt/) — a nationwide pure land tax, administratively cheap.
- [Site-value rating in Australia](/wiki/edwards-site-value-taxation-australia/) and [Australia](/wiki/australia/) — decades of municipal SVR, plus the [Victoria SVR/NAV comparison](/wiki/victoria-svr-nav-comparison/).
- [Germany](/wiki/germany/) and [Büttner on German land-tax capitalization](/wiki/buettner-german-land-tax-capitalization/) — capitalization evidence from the reform debate.

## Denmark — two studies in tension

- [Denmark](/wiki/denmark/) — the jurisdiction profile.
- [Danish Economic Councils on land taxes and prices](/wiki/dors-land-taxes-housing-prices/) — a quasi-experimental capitalization result.
- [Nielsson, Wroblewski & Yding on the Danish LVT](/wiki/nielsson-wroblewski-yding-danish-lvt/) — the counterpart study read against it.

## Resource rents and dividends

- [Norway](/wiki/norway/) and [Lie on the Norwegian oil fund](/wiki/lie-norwegian-oil-fund/) — the flagship capture success.
- [Alaska](/wiki/alaska/) and [Jones & Marinescu on the Permanent Fund Dividend](/wiki/jones-marinescu-alaska-pfd/) — the dividend that did not kill employment.
- [Acemoglu, Johnson & Robinson on Botswana](/wiki/acemoglu-johnson-robinson-botswana/) — diamond rent captured well, institutions the decisive variable.
- [Maricá, Brazil](/wiki/marica-brazil-basic-income/) — a municipal royalty-funded basic income.

## Capture ≠ cheap housing

- [Singapore](/wiki/singapore/) and [Hong Kong](/wiki/hong-kong/) — maximal land-rent capture alongside severe housing costs.
- [The "land capture didn't make housing cheap" objection](/wiki/land-capture-didnt-make-housing-cheap/) — why the two are distinct policy goals.

## The developing-world frontier

- [Bergeron, Tourek & Weigel on the DRC property tax](/wiki/bergeron-tourek-weigel-drc-property-tax/) — a randomized property-tax rollout in a low-capacity state.
- [Tubío & Reyes on the Loja, Ecuador LVT](/wiki/tubio-reyes-loja-ecuador-lvt/) — a municipal land-value tax evaluated in the field.
