## How to use this

You're writing a memo and someone will ask "where has this actually been done, and what happened?" This is that table — the **real record**, outcomes not aspirations, every row linked to the jurisdiction profile and the study behind it. The **caveat column is the point**: a staffer who can't state the honest limitation of a case gets caught out with it. Nothing here is stronger than its source; where the evidence is mixed or the case is a partial precedent, the table says so.

For *how* to design the policy, see the [Policymaker's Brief](/wiki/policymakers-brief/); for the graded state of the underlying claims, the [Evidence Dashboard](/wiki/evidence-dashboard/).

## Split-rate & land value taxation

| Jurisdiction | What was done | Measured result | The honest caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Pittsburgh, PA](/wiki/pittsburgh/) | Two-rate tax (land taxed ~5× buildings), 1913–2001 | A 1979–80 building-permit surge that [Oates & Schwab](/wiki/oates-schwab-pittsburgh/) isolated from the regional boom | The split rate was one factor among several; the city dropped it in 2001 after an assessment scandal, not an economic failure |
| [Pennsylvania](/wiki/pennsylvania/) (≈15+ cities) | Municipal two-rate taxation | Cross-city evidence of higher construction where the land rate is higher | Effects vary by city and are entangled with local conditions; not a clean single experiment |
| [Estonia](/wiki/estonia/) | Nationwide **pure land tax** since 1993 | Runs at national scale, administratively cheap; [Tomson](/wiki/tomson-estonia-lvt/) links it to higher density/construction vs. Latvia | Rates are low, so it's a proof of *administrability* more than of a high-rate tax's full effects |
| [Denmark](/wiki/denmark/) | Long-standing *grundskyld* land tax | [Danish Economic Councils](/wiki/dors-land-taxes-housing-prices/) find land-tax changes capitalize into lower land prices (quasi-experimental) | A [counterpart study](/wiki/nielsson-wroblewski-yding-danish-lvt/) finds a precise *zero* short-run price effect — the capitalization magnitude is genuinely contested |
| [Australia](/wiki/australia/) | Decades of municipal **site-value rating** | [Long SVR record](/wiki/edwards-site-value-taxation-australia/); the [Victoria SVR/NAV comparison](/wiki/victoria-svr-nav-comparison/) reads land-vs-property bases against each other | Municipal scale and gradual; not a national single-tax test |

## Resource-rent capture & dividends

| Jurisdiction | What was done | Measured result | The honest caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Norway](/wiki/norway/) | 78% petroleum rent tax + sovereign fund | [A $2-trillion fund](/wiki/lie-norwegian-oil-fund/) built without deterring extraction — the flagship capture success | The escape was **institutional** (a sterilising fund + a binding spending rule), not automatic from the tax |
| [Alaska](/wiki/alaska/) | Oil-rent **citizen's dividend** since 1982 | [Jones & Marinescu](/wiki/jones-marinescu-alaska-pfd/): the universal dividend had *no* negative employment effect | One [econometric study](/wiki/kozminski-baek-alaska-inequality/) finds it worsened measured inequality; direct poverty evidence is descriptive |
| [Botswana](/wiki/acemoglu-johnson-robinson-botswana/) | Public capture of diamond rent (Debswana) | Decades of top-tier growth with Dutch disease avoided — capture working in a poor country | The decisive variable was **institutions**, not the rent; success was in growth/finance, not across the board |
| [Maricá, Brazil](/wiki/marica-brazil-basic-income/) | Municipal oil-royalty basic income | Household income ≈+9%, consumption ≈+5% (NBER evaluation) | Modest effects, quasi-experimental; a middle-income *municipal* case, not a national program |

## The honest limits (cases that cut against the easy story)

| Jurisdiction | What it shows | Why a staffer needs it |
|---|---|---|
| [Singapore](/wiki/singapore/) & [Hong Kong](/wiki/hong-kong/) | Maximal land-rent capture **alongside** severe housing costs | Shows capture raises revenue but does **not** by itself make housing cheap — [affordability needs supply](/wiki/land-capture-didnt-make-housing-cheap/). Concede this before it's used against you. |
| [DRC](/wiki/bergeron-tourek-weigel-drc-property-tax/) | A **randomized** property-tax rollout in a low-capacity state | Real-world evidence that collection is buildable where capacity is thin — but also that state-capacity ceilings bind |
| [Loja, Ecuador](/wiki/tubio-reyes-loja-ecuador-lvt/) | A municipal land-value tax evaluated in the field | Developing-world implementation is feasible at the city level, with field-measured effects |

## The one-line version for the memo

The **revenue-and-efficiency** case is well-evidenced and has been run at scale (Estonia, the Nordic land taxes, Norway's fund). The **affordability** case runs through housing *supply*, and the flagship capture cities prove capture alone isn't enough. The **dividend** case has decades of operation (Alaska) and a spreading modern record (Maricá). Everywhere, the decisive variable is **institutional design**, not the mere fact of taxing rent — which is exactly what a good memo should say.
