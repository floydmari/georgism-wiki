## Overview

The **Land Value Increment Tax (LVIT)** is Taiwan's tax on the *increase* in a parcel's assessed land value between transactions, capturing the [unearned increment](/wiki/unearned-increment/) at the point it is realized rather than on a recurrent basis.[1] Its intellectual lineage runs to [Sun Yat-sen](/wiki/sun-yat-sen/), founder of the Republic of China, whose principle of "equalization of land rights" — directly influenced by Henry George — became constitutional doctrine and was implemented through the LVIT alongside a recurrent land value tax.[1] The Lincoln Institute's case study documents the system as one of the most explicit national applications of Georgist land-value-capture principles, designed to return socially created land value to the public and discourage speculative land holding.[1]
