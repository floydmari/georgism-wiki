## Definition

A **community land trust (CLT)** is a nonprofit organisation that owns **land** and leases it long-term to residents or businesses, who own the **buildings** on it. By separating ownership of land from improvements, a CLT captures the rising value of the land for the community rather than letting it accrue to individual owners — applying the Georgist land/building distinction at neighbourhood scale.

## How It Embodies Georgist Ideas

Because the CLT retains the land and charges a ground lease, the [unearned increment](/wiki/unearned-increment/) of rising land value is captured collectively (often used to keep housing permanently affordable) — a voluntary, local form of [land value capture](/wiki/land-value-capture/). The model descends partly from [Ebenezer Howard's](/wiki/ebenezer-howard/) garden cities and the [single-tax colonies](/wiki/single-tax-colonies/) like Fairhope, which pioneered community land ownership with ground rents.
