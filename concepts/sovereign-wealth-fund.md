A sovereign wealth fund is a mechanism for collecting and distributing resource rents, typically capitalized from natural resource revenues and used to provide ongoing dividends or public services. The Common Wealth Fund proposal for Canada outlines a detailed model for such a fund, covering capitalization sources, governance structures, dividend distribution, and international precedents. The concept is referenced in Canadian resource-rent analyses as a vehicle for transforming common wealth from land and resources into a permanent public asset, with provincial examples including Alberta's heritage fund and similar mechanisms across Canadian provinces.
