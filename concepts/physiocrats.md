## Overview

The **Physiocrats** were the first organized school of economic thought, active in France in the mid-to-late 18th century and led by the court physician **François Quesnay** (1694–1774), the statesman-economist **A.R.J. Turgot** (1727–1781), and the publicist **Victor de Riqueti, marquis de Mirabeau** ("the elder Mirabeau"). The name derives from the Greek *phýsis* ("nature") and *kràtos* ("power"): they held that an economy's wealth flowed ultimately from the land, and that only the agricultural sector produced a genuine surplus — the **produit net** (net product) — over and above the costs of cultivation.[1]

Quesnay set out this vision in his *Tableau économique* (1758), a "zigzag" circular-flow diagram tracing how output and spending moved among three classes: landowners, the productive (agricultural) class, and a **"sterile" class** of manufacturers and others who, in the Physiocratic model, merely consumed what they produced and left no surplus for the next period.[1] Because in their theory only land generated a true surplus, the Physiocrats argued that all the miscellaneous taxes, tolls, and regulations burdening the French economy should be swept away and replaced by a single tax falling on that surplus — the **impôt unique**, a single tax on the net product of land. Henry George reports that the elder Mirabeau "ranked the proposition of Quesnay, to substitute one single tax on rent (the *impôt unique*) for all other taxes, as a discovery equal in utility to the invention of writing or the substitution of the use of money for barter."[2a] Quesnay also coined the phrase "*laissez-faire, laissez-passer*," and the school's attack on the mercantilism of Louis XV's finance minister Colbert anticipated much of classical free-trade economics.[1] Turgot, as intendant of Limoges and later finance minister (1774–1776), attempted to put Physiocratic reform into practice, and his analysis of capital, competition, and diminishing returns anticipated Adam Smith.[3]

## Relevance to Georgism

The Physiocrats are the most direct pre-classical ancestors of the Georgist [single tax](/wiki/single-tax/). The lineage runs **Physiocracy → the classical critique → George**:

- **The idea George revived.** In *Progress and Poverty*, Book VIII, Ch. IV ("Indorsements and Objections"), George explicitly credits the school: "The French Economists of the last century, headed by Quesnay and Turgot, proposed just what I have proposed, that all taxation should be abolished save a tax upon the value of land."[2] He acknowledged reaching his own conclusion independently — "Without knowing anything of Quesnay or his doctrines, I have reached the same practical conclusion by a route which cannot be disputed"[2] — but recognized the Physiocrats had grasped "the fundamental relation between land and labor which has since been lost sight of."[2]
- **The classical hand-off.** [Adam Smith](/wiki/adam-smith/) met the Physiocrats in France and admired them, while criticizing the doctrine that manufacturing is "sterile"; through Smith and Ricardo the analysis of rent passed into classical economics, where George picked it up.[1] The Physiocrats thus form the first link in the chain that produced the [law of rent](/wiki/law-of-rent/).
- **Where George honestly differed.** George taxed **land rent** — the value of location and the [unearned increment](/wiki/unearned-increment/) — not merely agricultural produce, and he rejected the Physiocrats' claim that only agriculture is "productive." He treated their peculiar terminology cautiously, saying he knew their doctrines "only at second hand" and could not tell how far their ideas about "agriculture being the only productive avocation" were "erroneous apprehensions, or mere peculiarities of terminology" (Book VIII, Ch. IV).[2] The Physiocrats' surplus was tied to farming; George's rent arises wherever fixed natural opportunities command a premium — a broader and, in modern terms, sounder basis for the same single-tax conclusion (see the [rent gradient](/wiki/economic-rent/): land is the clean, fixed-supply case).
