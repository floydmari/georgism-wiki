The margin of production is a core Georgist concept holding that wages are set by productivity at the margin of cultivation—the least productive land currently in use—rather than by average productivity across all land. As explained by Lars Doucet on the Dwarkesh Podcast, this margin establishes the upper bound on the rent landowners can charge: any output above what workers could earn at the margin accrues to landowners as rent. Henry George developed this argument extensively in *Progress and Poverty*, where it serves as a central theoretical pillar explaining why rising land values depress wages even as overall productivity increases.
