Optimal city size refers to the population level at which the marginal benefits of spreading public-goods costs over more residents equal the marginal costs of increased commuting and crowding. Arnott and Stiglitz (1979) formally derived the conditions under which the Henry George Theorem holds at optimal city size, showing that aggregate land rents can exactly finance the optimal level of public goods. Arnott (2004) later examined whether this theoretical construct provides a practical empirical guide, noting complications from heterogeneity and the integer problem. Kanemoto, Ohkawara, and Suzuki (1996) tested whether Japanese cities, including Tokyo, exceed their optimal size using an HGT-derived framework.
