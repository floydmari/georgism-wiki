## Overview

Dominic Frisby is a British comedian, author, and voice actor who doubles as a financial writer — "probably the world's only financial writer and comedian," by his own description. He is best known in finance circles as *MoneyWeek*'s long-time commentator on gold, commodities, forex, and bitcoin, and writes the *Flying Frisby* newsletter. Within the Georgist world he is a notable popular advocate of land value taxation in the UK, reaching general audiences that academic work does not.

## Daylight Robbery

Frisby's 2019 book ***Daylight Robbery: How Tax Shaped Our Past and Will Change Our Future*** (Portfolio Penguin) is a popular history of taxation that gives land value taxation a sympathetic hearing, tracing how tax systems have shaped civilisations and arguing for reform toward simpler, harder-to-avoid bases. The book is tracked on this wiki's popular-framing narrative [the tax you can't dodge](/wiki/the-tax-you-cant-dodge/), where Frisby's central selling point for LVT is that land "can't be hidden in a briefcase or flown to Switzerland" — it is simply there, making it the tax that is hardest to avoid.

## Explainers and Reach

Frisby's accessible explainers — including a widely-viewed video, *"A Simple Guide to Land Value Tax,"* catalogued among this wiki's [Georgism video explainers](/wiki/georgism-video-explainers/) — present the LVT case with clarity and humour. He frames land value taxation as a commonsense reform that taxes unearned wealth while freeing productive activity, helping carry the idea into mainstream British political debate. His comedy background (he performs satirical songs and stand-up) and his large finance-and-crypto readership give his LVT advocacy an unusual reach outside the traditional Georgist audience.
