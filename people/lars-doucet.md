## Overview

Lars Doucet is a Norwegian-Texan game developer, writer, and land-assessment entrepreneur who became one of the most influential popularisers of Georgism in the 2020s. His work reframed land value taxation for a technically literate online audience and is widely credited with reviving interest in Henry George's ideas in Silicon Valley, rationalist, and effective-altruist communities.

## Contributions

- **ACX Book Review Contest win (2021)** — Doucet won first place in Scott Alexander's *Astral Codex Ten* Book Review Contest with a review of *[Progress and Poverty](/wiki/progress-and-poverty/)*, [published 16 April 2021 and announced as the winner on 10 July 2021](/wiki/doucet-acx-book-review/). The win led Alexander to invite Doucet back as a guest author, launching the *Does Georgism Work?* series that December.
- **"Does Georgism Work?"** — His [three-part Astral Codex Ten series](/wiki/doucet-does-georgism-work/) (2021–22) examined the main empirical objections to LVT (magnitude, incidence, assessment).
- ***[Land Is a Big Deal](/wiki/land-is-a-big-deal/)*** (2022, Shack Simple Press) — A 400-page book expanding the series into the most accessible modern introduction to Georgism, synthesising twelve competing US land-value estimates ($19–65 trillion range), the Danish natural-experiment evidence on LVT capitalization, and modern mass-appraisal methods; praised by Vitalik Buterin, Scott Alexander, Noah Smith, and Richard Florida.
- **[Center for Land Economics](/wiki/center-for-land-economics/)** — Doucet co-founded this US nonprofit with Greg Miller in **February 2025**. It builds practical land-assessment tools (the open-source LVTShift modelling platform) and has published empirical studies of vacant-land undervaluation in Baltimore and Maryland, directly addressing the assessment objection; the Center won a 2025 J.M.K. Innovation Prize.

## Listening & Watching

Doucet is a frequent audio/video presence: his long-form conversation on the
[Dwarkesh Podcast](/wiki/dwarkesh-doucet-podcast/) is a widely recommended single-sitting
introduction to Georgism, and he appears in several of the
[Georgism video explainers](/wiki/georgism-video-explainers/) circulating on YouTube.

## Significance

Doucet represents the contemporary, empirically minded wing of Georgism: less concerned with George's 19th-century rhetoric than with whether the policy survives modern scrutiny on data and implementation. His answer — that it largely does — brought a new generation to the movement.
