## Overview

**Henry George Jr.** (November 3, 1862 – November 14, 1916) was the eldest son of
economist [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/) — reporter, two-term U.S. Congressman,
and author of the standard filial biography of his father. Born in Sacramento,
California, he began work in a printing office at sixteen, moved with his family to
Brooklyn in 1880, and worked as a *Brooklyn Eagle* reporter before accompanying his
father as secretary on George's 1884 lecture tour of Great Britain. He went on to
editorial posts with the London *Truth*, the *North American Review*, and the
Georgist weekly *The Standard* (managing editor, 1887–1891), and later worked as a
Washington correspondent for a syndicate of Western newspapers.[1]

## Congressional Career

Elected as a Democrat from New York, George Jr. served two terms in the U.S. House
of Representatives — the 62nd and 63rd Congresses, from March 4, 1911 to March 3,
1915 — representing successive New York districts. As a Congressman he gave
speeches and sponsored bills favoring a single tax on land values. He did not seek
reelection in 1914 and afterward devoted himself to literary work until his death
in Washington, D.C. in 1916; he is interred at Green-Wood Cemetery, Brooklyn — the
same cemetery where his father and sister Jennie are buried.[1][2]

While in Congress, he made a publicized visit to [Vancouver](/wiki/vancouver/) in
October 1911, at the height of that city's land-only municipal tax system, to
promote his father's economic theories; he was reported as remarking that
"Vancouver is regarded as an advanced city, as a city leading the way."[3]

## *Life of Henry George* (1900)

After his father's fatal 1897 mayoral campaign, George Jr. spent three years
compiling family papers, letters, and his own memory into ***The Life of Henry
George*** (Doubleday & McClure, 1900) — the standard primary biography, and the
source nearly every later account of George's life builds on or argues with,
including [Barker's 1955 academic biography](/wiki/barker-henry-george-biography/).
The book is public domain and its complete text is hosted on this wiki; see
[The Life of Henry George](/wiki/life-of-henry-george/) for the full work and
[sources/publicdomain/life-of-henry-george.md](https://github.com/floydmari/georgism-wiki/blob/main/sources/publicdomain/life-of-henry-george.md)
for the raw transcription. It is a sympathetic, filial account — documentary gold on
dates and correspondence, but cited on this wiki with that loyalty noted (D-claims
where the son characterizes his father).

## Nuances and Limits

- **Primary-source status, not independent secondary scholarship.** Wherever this
  wiki cites George Jr. for episodes in his father's life (the 1886 and 1897 mayoral
  campaigns, the Irish Land War reporting, the "flash of insight" account), the
  citation is to a family member's memoir, not a disinterested historian — treated
  as A-claims for quoted primary material (letters, speeches, vote totals) and
  D-claims for interpretation.
- **His own political career is comparatively thin in the record here.** Beyond the
  congressional basics and the Vancouver visit, this page does not yet cover his
  1897 mayoral candidacy (he was put forward, with little support, to carry his
  father's ticket after George Sr.'s death) in depth — see
  [The Last Campaign, 1897](/wiki/1897-nyc-mayoral-campaign/).
