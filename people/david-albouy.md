David Albouy is an urban economist who has made multiple contributions to the land-value measurement and urban-economics literature. His 2018 paper with Ehrlich and Shin, *Metropolitan Land Values*, provides detailed estimates of land values across U.S. metropolitan areas. His 2016 paper, *What Are Cities Worth? Land Rents, Local Productivity, and the Total Value of Amenities*, estimates the relationship between land rents, local productivity, and urban amenities, providing empirical foundations for understanding how much of urban value derives from land versus other factors. Together, these contributions represent sustained influence in the estimation of urban land rents and their role in local economies.
