Edward Glaeser is a major urban economist whose research has made sustained contributions to the land and housing economics literature. His 2013 AER paper, *A Nation of Gamblers: Real Estate Speculation and American History*, documents the long history of real estate speculation in the United States, examining how speculative booms and busts have shaped American economic development. Glaeser's broader body of work on housing supply, land-use restrictions, and urban growth has been influential in Georgist and urban-economics discourse, particularly regarding how regulatory constraints on land use affect housing affordability and spatial allocation.
