Richard T. Ely was an American economist who helped establish "land economics" as a distinct academic field in the early twentieth century. In Mason Gaffney's essay *Neo-classical Economics as a Stratagem Against Henry George*, Ely is discussed as a key institutional figure whose framing of land and taxation issues served to marginalize Henry George's single-tax program within the economics profession.
