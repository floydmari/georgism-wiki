## Overview

Louis Freeland Post (1849–1928) was the single-tax movement's leading journalist-
organizer after [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/) himself: editor of **The Public**
(1898–1913), the movement's national weekly, and George's defender in set-piece
debates — including the 1903 Cooper Union debate against
[John Bates Clark](/wiki/john-bates-clark/) on the single tax.[1] With Julia
Lathrop he helped found the U.S. Children's Bureau, and as Assistant Secretary of
Labor under Wilson he canceled the bulk of the 1920 Palmer-raid deportation
warrants, surviving an impeachment attempt for it — the movement's most senior
federal officeholder.[2] He worked the [Joseph Fels](/wiki/joseph-fels/) Lecture
Bureau circuit alongside Nearing and Howe
([Gaffney's account](/wiki/gaffney-harrison-corruption-of-economics/), essay Ch. 3).[1]
