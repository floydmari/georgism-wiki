Peter Mieszkowski was a public finance economist who developed the 'new view' (or capital-tax view) of property tax incidence in his 1972 paper, *The Property Tax: An Excise Tax or a Profits Tax?* This work reframed the property tax as a tax on capital rather than a benefit tax, becoming a foundational contribution to the property tax incidence literature. His framework was later reformulated and extended in collaboration with George Zodrow, whose 2001 paper *The Property Tax as a Capital Tax: A Room with Three Views* situates Mieszkowski's new view alongside the traditional and benefit views.
