## Overview

Michael Davitt founded the **Irish National Land League** and, alongside Charles Stewart
Parnell, led the mass agitation of the **[Irish Land War](/wiki/irish-land-war/)** (1879–82)
against evictions and rack-renting by landlords, centred on the "Three Fs" (fair rent, fixity of
tenure, free sale).[1] Davitt engaged seriously with **[Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/)**'s
ideas during George's 1881–82 tour and reporting on the Land War, and the two corresponded and
collaborated for a period — but Davitt's own preferred remedy leaned toward state land
nationalization, at times combined with peasant-proprietorship politics, diverging from
George's tax-based single-tax approach set out in *[The Land Question](/wiki/the-land-question/)*.[1][2]
The historian Andrew Phemister's *Land and Liberalism* (2023) is the fullest modern academic
treatment of Davitt's relationship with George and the Irish Land War's place in Anglo-American
land reform politics.[1]
