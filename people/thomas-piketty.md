Thomas Piketty is a French economist best known for *Capital in the Twenty-First Century* (2014), which documents long-run trends in wealth and income inequality and argues that the rate of return on capital tends to exceed economic growth, causing capital's share to rise. His framework has been critically extended by Georgist-leaning economists who decompose "capital" into land and structure components, finding that rising land values—not productive capital—drive much of the observed increase in capital's share. The wiki's research page on this work is [Piketty — Capital in the 21st Century](/wiki/piketty-capital-21st-century/).
