## Overview

Karl Fitzgerald is an Australian Georgist campaigner, researcher, and broadcaster, for many years the most visible public voice of [Prosper Australia](/wiki/prosper-australia/) and its associated body [Earthsharing Australia](/wiki/earthsharing-australia/).

## Contributions

- **Speculative Vacancies research.** Fitzgerald led Prosper's pioneering [speculative vacancy](/wiki/speculative-vacancy/) studies, using water-consumption data to reveal how much housing sits deliberately empty in Melbourne — original empirical work that few others have matched and that fed into the case for vacancy taxes.
- **Total Resource Rents of Australia.** He authored *[Total Resource Rents of Australia](/wiki/total-resource-rents-australia/)* (2013), an estimate of the economic rent from land and natural resources available as a public revenue base — carried, with caveats, on this wiki's [land rent could fund government](/wiki/land-rent-could-fund-government/) page.
- **Real Estate 4 Ransom.** He co-directed (with Gavin Emmanuel) the 2012 documentary *[Real Estate 4 Ransom](/wiki/real-estate-4-ransom/)*, shot over five years, which reads the global financial crisis as substantially a land-speculation crisis — the Georgist movement's most-circulated film.
- **Renegade Economists.** He hosted the long-running *[Renegade Economists](/wiki/renegade-economists-podcast/)* radio show and podcast (600+ episodes), bringing land-tax, banking, and monopoly analysis to a broad Australian audience.
- **Advocacy and media.** Through Prosper and Earthsharing he has been a tireless populariser of Georgist economics, and his work anchors the modern Australian strand of [geoism](/wiki/geoism/).

## Significance

Fitzgerald's combination of original empirical research (speculative vacancies, resource-rent estimation) and sustained popular media (film and a 600-episode podcast) made Prosper Australia one of the most productive Georgist organisations of the early 21st century and kept land-value taxation a live topic in Australian policy debate.
