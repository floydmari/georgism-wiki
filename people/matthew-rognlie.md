Matthew Rognlie is an economist whose work has had sustained influence on the land-economics and capital-share debate. His 2015 paper, *Deciphering the Fall and Rise in the Net Capital Share*, demonstrated that the secular rise in the capital income share is driven almost entirely by housing rather than by productive capital, a finding with direct implications for Georgist arguments about land rent. His 2014 note, *A Note on Piketty and Diminishing Returns to Capital*, challenged Thomas Piketty's framework by arguing that diminishing returns to capital undermine the prediction of an indefinitely rising capital share. Rognlie's decomposition of capital income into housing and non-housing components has been widely cited in Georgist discourse as evidence that rising inequality is fundamentally a land-rent phenomenon.
