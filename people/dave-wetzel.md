## Overview

Dave Wetzel (1942–2024) was a British bus-industry trade unionist turned transport official and long-time [land value tax](/wiki/land-value-tax/) campaigner. He served as the first Vice-Chairman of Transport for London for eight years (2000–2008) under Mayor Ken Livingstone, and was also Chair of London Buses (2000–01) and of TfL's Safety, Health and Environment Committee.[1][2] He was a long-standing officer of the [Labour Land Campaign](/wiki/labour-land-campaign/) and President of the [International Union for Land Value Taxation](/wiki/international-union-lvt/).[2]

Wetzel argued for land value taxation, following [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/), as a fairer and more efficient way to fund public transport than fares or general taxation, on the grounds that transport investment itself raises the value of land near stations and routes.[3] Fred Harrison's *[Ricardo's Law](/wiki/harrison-ricardos-law/)* (2006) documents Wetzel pressing this case, alongside Mayor Livingstone, in official transport-funding debates including submissions to the UK government's Lyons Inquiry into local government finance.[4] He was also an advocate of [congestion pricing](/wiki/congestion-pricing/) for London as a complementary land/road-rent capture mechanism.
