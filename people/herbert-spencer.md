Herbert Spencer is the central subject of Henry George's *A Perplexed Philosopher* (1892), a sustained critique of Spencer's reversal on the question of land rights. George argued that Spencer's earlier writings supported the equal right of all to land, and devoted the book to challenging Spencer's later accommodation of private land ownership. The work positions Spencer as a pivotal figure in Georgist discourse on the ethics of land property and the consistency of equal-rights principles.
