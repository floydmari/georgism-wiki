## Overview

Joseph Fels (1853–1914), the Philadelphia soap manufacturer (Fels-Naptha), was the
single-tax movement's great financier: the **Joseph Fels Fund** (from 1909)
committed his fortune to putting [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/)'s program into
practice, funding state single-tax campaigns
([Oregon's initiative fights](/wiki/single-tax-narrative/)), the Philadelphia
lecture bureau that employed Louis F. Post, Scott Nearing, and Frederic Howe
([Gaffney's account](/wiki/gaffney-harrison-corruption-of-economics/), essay
Ch. 3), and land-colony experiments in Britain.[1] His money is why the movement's
1909–1916 peak — the [People's Budget](/wiki/1909-peoples-budget/) era through the
California initiative — had professional organization behind it.[1]
