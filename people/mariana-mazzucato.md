## Overview

Mariana Mazzucato (b. 1968) is an Italian-American-British economist at University College London, known for *The Value of Everything* (2018) and her work on the role of the state in innovation. Her central theme — the difference between **creating** value and **extracting** it — directly revives the classical and Georgist concern with economic rent.

## Relevance to Georgism

Mazzucato argues that modern economies increasingly reward **value extraction** — rent-seeking through finance, intellectual property, and land — over genuine value creation, and that national accounting wrongly counts much extraction as production. With Josh Ryan-Collins and Giorgos Gouzoulis she authored ["Mapping Modern Economic Rents"](/wiki/mazzucato-mapping-rents/) (2023), a framework spanning land, finance, and digital-platform rents.

## Significance

Mazzucato brings the language of [rent-seeking](/wiki/rent-seeking/) into mainstream policy discourse, providing contemporary intellectual support for taxing rent rather than production — even where she does not frame it as Georgism.
