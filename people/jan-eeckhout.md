Jan Eeckhout is an economist whose research program on market power, inequality, and markups has had sustained influence on the discourse surrounding corporate rents and their macroeconomic implications. He co-authored *The Rise of Market Power and the Macroeconomic Implications* (De Loecker, Eeckhout & Unger, 2020), a core paper documenting the secular increase in firm markups and its consequences for labor's share of income. He further developed these themes in *The Profit Paradox: How Thriving Firms Threaten the Future of Work* (2021), a trade book exploring how market power enables firms to extract rents at the expense of workers. His work is cited by researchers including Autor, Zucman, and the IMF in analyses of rising corporate profits and rent-seeking.
