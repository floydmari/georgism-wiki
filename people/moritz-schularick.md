Moritz Schularick is a financial historian who co-authored *No Price Like Home: Global House Prices, 1870–2012* (Knoll, Schularick & Steger, 2017), an influential American Economic Review paper documenting the long-run trajectory of global house prices. The study traces housing prices across 17 advanced economies over 140 years, finding that house prices have risen dramatically relative to other prices, with implications for understanding the growing role of land and housing in modern economies. Schularick's broader work on credit and housing cycles intersects with land economics by providing the historical empirical foundation for analyzing housing as a store of wealth.
