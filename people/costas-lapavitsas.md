## Overview

Costas Lapavitsas (born January 20, 1961) is a Marxist political economist and professor of economics at SOAS, University of London, where he has taught since 1999, specializing in monetary theory and the political economy of money and finance.[1] He earned a master's degree at the London School of Economics (1982) and a PhD at Birkbeck College, University of London (1986), and in 2007 founded Research on Money and Finance, an international network of political economists studying finance and financialization.[1] He was briefly a member of the Hellenic Parliament for the left-wing Syriza party, elected in the January 2015 general election and serving until he defected to the Popular Unity party in August 2015 amid the Greek debt-crisis negotiations.[1]

Lapavitsas's account of "financialization of capitalism," developed in his book *Profiting Without Producing: How Finance Exploits Us All* (Verso, 2013) and the article the wiki's [research page](/wiki/lapavitsas-financialization/) summarizes, argues that financial profit increasingly arises from **financial expropriation** — extraction of profit directly from wage and salary income, including via mortgage debt — rather than only from the exploitation of labor in production.[2] This is a labour-value-theoretic, Marxist framework distinct from the wiki's classical land-rent analysis, cited on the [FIRE sector](/wiki/fire-sector/) concept page as a heterodox voice alongside Michael Hudson, and discussed as a contrast case (not corroboration) on the [finance-growth-is-land-credit](/wiki/finance-growth-is-land-credit/) problem page, since his mechanism locates extraction in the debtor's wage stream rather than in appreciating land collateral.[2]
