## Overview

Walter Rybeck was an American journalist, editor, and Georgist writer, born in Wheeling, West Virginia. He worked as a reporter for the Columbus Citizen, an editor at the Dayton Daily News, and Washington Bureau Chief for Cox Newspapers before moving into public-interest policy work: Assistant Director for the National Commission on Urban Problems (1967), editorial director at the Urban Institute, and aide to Congressmen Henry Reuss and William Coyne.[1][2] He later founded his own consultancy, the Center for Public Dialog, and wrote *Re-Solving the Economic Puzzle: How to Save the Enterprise System and Trigger Sustainable Economic Recovery* (2011), applying Georgist economic-justice ideas to housing, land speculation, and property-tax reform.[1][2] He died of pneumonia on May 3, 2021, in Silver Spring, Maryland, at age 96.[1][2]

Rybeck's byline connects to two works already in this wiki. He wrote the preface to the Citizens' Housing and Planning Council's May 1960 study of how New York City's 1920s building-tax exemption broke the postwar housing deadlock — a report financed by the [Robert Schalkenbach Foundation](/wiki/schalkenbach-foundation/), whose strongest causal claims the wiki attributes to his preface rather than the report's documentary body.[3] He also provided editorial assistance on Robert V. Andelson and James M. Dawsey's [*From Wasteland to Promised Land*](/wiki/from-wasteland-to-promised-land/) (1992), which applies George's land-value-tax thesis to Latin American liberation theology.[4]
