## Overview

**Jeffery Johnson Smith** (publishing under the byline Jeffery J. Smith) is a Georgist writer and long-serving chief editor of [Progress.org](https://www.progress.org/), the online publication associated with the modern geoist movement.[1] In his 2020 book *Counting Bounty: The Quest to Know the Worth of Earth* (Trine Day LLC), Smith produced an original synthesis estimating the total value of land and natural resources in the United States, building on the transaction-based land-value work of David Albouy and colleagues and adding categories such as farmland and federal lands.[2][3] Lars Doucet's *[Land is a Big Deal](/wiki/land-is-a-big-deal/)* (2022) surveys twelve US land-value estimation methods in its Ch. 14 comparison table and lists Smith's figure at roughly **$44 trillion** — the highest of the estimates tabulated there, above the Federal Reserve/Yglesias cost-approach estimate (~$24T) and the Larson (2015) hedonic estimate (~$33T).[2] That figure feeds the wiki's broader claim that [land rent could fund a large share of government](/wiki/land-rent-could-fund-government/).

Smith's estimate is an advocate's aggregation rather than an official government or peer-reviewed academic figure, and Doucet's own comparison table presents it alongside more conservative estimates rather than as a settled consensus number.[2] A primary-source, chapter-and-page account of Smith's exact methodology in *Counting Bounty* — beyond the secondhand summary in Doucet (2022) — has not been consulted for this page.
