Wallace E. Oates was a public finance economist who made foundational contributions to the land-economics and public-finance literature. His 1969 study on the effects of property taxes and local public spending on property values established the empirical capitalization literature, demonstrating that local public goods and tax rates capitalize into property values. With Robert M. Schwab, he co-authored the landmark 1997 study of Pittsburgh's split-rate property tax system, examining the impact of urban land taxation on the city's development. These contributions span decades of sustained influence on property tax incidence and land value taxation research.
