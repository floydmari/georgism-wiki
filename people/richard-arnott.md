Richard Arnott is an urban economist who co-authored the canonical formalization of the Henry George Theorem with Joseph Stiglitz in their 1979 paper on aggregate land rents, expenditure on public goods, and optimal city size. He later authored a 2004 retrospective assessing whether the Henry George Theorem provides a practical guide to optimal city size, examining heterogeneity issues and the integer problem that complicate its empirical application. His contributions to land economics span the theoretical relationship between public goods, land rents, and urban population levels.
