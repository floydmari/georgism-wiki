## What It Is

*The Life of Henry George* is the primary biography of [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/), written by his eldest son and campaign companion **Henry George Jr.** and published in 1900, three years after George's death. The author had his father's papers, letters, and the family's memory at hand, and organized the life into three periods the book itself names: *Formation of the Character* (1839–1869), *Formulation of the Philosophy* (1869–1879), and *Propagation of the Philosophy* (1879–1897).

Nearly every later biography — including [Barker's 1955 academic life](/wiki/barker-henry-george-biography/) — builds on or argues with this one. It is documentary gold on dates, letters, and campaign detail, and, as a son's account, predictably gentle on George's failures; cite it as a primary source with that loyalty in mind.

## Why It Matters Here

- It is the source of record for George's life chronology: the Philadelphia childhood, the voyage to India and Australia before the mast, the San Francisco printing and newspaper years, the 1871 pamphlet *Our Land and Land Policy*, the writing of [*Progress and Poverty*](/wiki/progress-and-poverty/), the Irish Land War reporting, the [1886 New York mayoralty campaign](/wiki/1886-nyc-mayoral-election/), and the fatal 1897 campaign.
- Its texture — letters quoted at length, campaign scenes witnessed first-hand — is exactly the primary record a definitive reference should carry rather than merely summarize.
