Canada is a country with substantial economic rents from land and natural resources, estimated at approximately $421 billion in total rents with roughly $241 billion potentially collectible, according to a 2023 national-level analysis. A 2024 distributional study modeled a national Canadian land value tax, estimating $5.824 trillion in national land value and $242 billion in potential revenue, while analyzing provincial constitutional constraints and income-decile distributional impacts. The Common Wealth Fund proposal examines Canada's resource wealth across provinces including Alberta, Quebec, Newfoundland and Labrador, the Northwest Territories, Saskatchewan, and British Columbia, addressing constitutional jurisdiction over resources and mineral wealth loss.
