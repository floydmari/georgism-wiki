Alaska is the U.S. state that has operated a permanent universal resource-rent dividend since 1982 through the Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD), making it a key real-world case study for Georgist and basic income researchers. Jones and Marinescu (2022) studied the labor market impacts of the PFD, finding evidence about the effects of universal and permanent cash transfers. Goldsmith (2002) examined the PFD as an experiment in wealth distribution, documenting its effects on inequality. The Widerquist and Howard (2012) edited volume provides a multi-disciplinary assessment of the PFD's suitability as a model for other jurisdictions, covering the state's fiscal structure, oil economy, and income distribution effects.
