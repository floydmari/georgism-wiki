Norway is a Nordic country whose Government Pension Fund Global (commonly called the oil fund) is widely cited as the leading example of a sovereign wealth fund built from resource rents. In the comparative literature on resource dividends, Norway's model—saving and budgeting resource revenues rather than distributing them directly—is contrasted with Alaska's Permanent Fund Dividend, which pays a per-capita dividend to residents; the Goldsmith study notes that Norway pays no such per-capita dividend. The wiki covers the Alaska dividend model and sovereign wealth fund concept in related pages.
