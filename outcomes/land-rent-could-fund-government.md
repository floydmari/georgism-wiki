## The Claim

The total economic rent of land and natural resources is large enough to fund a substantial portion of government spending, potentially replacing many taxes on labour and capital.

## The Evidence and the Debate

- [Gaffney (2009)](/wiki/gaffney-hidden-taxable-capacity/) argues US land rent is "enough and to spare," once hidden rent categories and the [ATCOR](/wiki/atcor/) effect (removing other taxes raises land values) are counted.
- More conservative estimates put currently measured land rent at a smaller share of GDP, sufficient to fund a meaningful but not total replacement of existing taxes.

The gap comes from **what counts as rent** and whether one includes the base-expanding effect of abolishing distortionary taxes.

## Strength of Evidence

**Contested.** That land rent is *large* is well established; *how large relative to government* depends heavily on method. Honest framing: enough to be a major revenue source, with full replacement an open question.
