## Overview

The Robert Schalkenbach Foundation (New York, founded 1925 from the bequest of
printer Robert Schalkenbach) is the Georgist movement's publishing institution: it
has kept [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/)'s complete works in print for a
century — the editions of [*Progress and Poverty*](/wiki/progress-and-poverty/),
[*Social Problems*](/wiki/social-problems/), and
[*The Land Question*](/wiki/the-land-question/) cited across this wiki — and funds
Georgist research and education, including the *American Journal of Economics and
Sociology*.[1] Alongside the
[Progress and Poverty Institute](/wiki/progress-and-poverty-institute/) (the former
Henry George Foundation of Canada renamed) it is one of the movement's two durable
institutional pillars in North America.
