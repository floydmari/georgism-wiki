## Overview

**Prosper Australia**, with roots reaching back to **1890**, is Australia's foremost Georgist research and advocacy organisation. Operating from Melbourne, it has kept land value taxation a live policy issue in a country with an unusually strong land-tax tradition.

## Work

- **[*Total Resource Rents of Australia*](/wiki/total-resource-rents-australia/) (2013)** — Karl Fitzgerald's line-by-line national rent inventory (23.6% of GDP), the modern movement's most-cited revenue-capacity study and the methodological template for Common Wealth Canada's 2023 Canadian counterpart.
- **[Speculative Vacancies research](/wiki/prosper-speculative-vacancies/)** — Prosper's long-running report series measures empty housing in Melbourne from water-meter data rather than survey or listing counts. The 11th report (2024) found **5.2% of Melbourne dwellings vacant or barely used in 2023 — one in twenty homes** — equivalent to more than two and a half years of new construction sitting idle, and argues vacancy is only "the tip of the iceberg" of broader land banking by developers.
- **Advocacy and education** — it campaigns for land value taxation and against property-speculation subsidies, and (through its outreach arm Earthsharing) ran the *[Renegade Economists](/wiki/renegade-economists-podcast/)* radio show and podcast — roughly **600 episodes on Melbourne community station 3CR from September 2007 to November 2021**, closing on the passage of Victoria's Windfall Gains Tax — plus a structured 12-part [Georgist Podcourse](/wiki/earthsharing-podcourse/) introducing land and rent economics.

## Significance

Prosper Australia is a model of evidence-driven Georgist advocacy, supplying original empirical research (notably on vacancy) that few other organisations produce.
