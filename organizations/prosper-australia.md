## Overview

**Prosper Australia**, with roots reaching back to **1890**, is Australia's foremost Georgist research and advocacy organisation. Operating from Melbourne, it has kept land value taxation a live policy issue in a country with an unusually strong land-tax tradition.

## Work

- **[*Total Resource Rents of Australia*](/wiki/total-resource-rents-australia/) (2013)** — Karl Fitzgerald's line-by-line national rent inventory (23.6% of GDP), the modern movement's most-cited revenue-capacity study and the methodological template for Common Wealth Canada's 2023 Canadian counterpart.
- **Speculative Vacancies research** — Prosper's pioneering studies use water-usage and other data to estimate how much housing sits **deliberately vacant**, documenting the speculative withholding of land and property that LVT is designed to discourage.
- **Advocacy and education** — it campaigns for land value taxation and against property-speculation subsidies, and (through its outreach arm Earthsharing) ran the *[Renegade Economists](/wiki/renegade-economists-podcast/)* podcast and Georgist courses.

## Significance

Prosper Australia is a model of evidence-driven Georgist advocacy, supplying original empirical research (notably on vacancy) that few other organisations produce.
