## Overview

The **Henry George Foundation of Canada** is Canada's long-standing Georgist organization, based in Ottawa and operating online as **earthsharing.ca**. It describes itself as "part of an international network of organizations that work to popularize the thought of 19th century American economist, Henry George," with three stated goals — "a just society, a green society and a prosperous society" — and frames that network as "an interconnected think tank advocating financing governments by capturing 'economic rent' in lieu of taxes on jobs, business and goods and services."[1]

Corporations Canada records show **The Henry George Foundation of Canada** federally incorporated on **December 1, 2014** (corporation #9104593), registered in Ottawa, with directors listed as Glenn Grignon, Francis K. Peddle, and Brendan Hennigan.[3] [VERIFY: whether the organization operated earlier under a prior incorporation or informal structure — the 2014 record documents only the current federal entity.]

## Work

- **Economic-rent education.** Its concise "Economic Rent" article defines rent as "unearned income, windfall profit, or … revenue without a corresponding cost of production" that "exists because of the existence of the community, and should accrue to that community," and argues rent recovery "does not inhibit extraction, use or economic activity."[2]
- **Earthsharing Economics Primer** and explainer pages on Henry George, [land value tax](/wiki/land-value-tax/) mechanics (worked household, business, and farm scenarios), and the [18-year land cycle](/wiki/18-year-land-cycle/), which features on its homepage.[1]
- **Reviews and resources.** It publishes book reviews and commentary (e.g., Francis K. Peddle's November 2024 review of Patrick Condon's *Broken City*) and hosts movement documents such as the Green Party of Ontario's 2010 "Proposed Government of Canada Budget Based on Rent Recovery."[1][4]

## Significance

The Foundation's practical importance to recent Canadian rent-capture research is as a **source of definitions and benchmarks**: [Common Wealth Canada](/wiki/common-wealth-canada/)'s *[Natural Common Wealth and Economic Rent in Canada](/wiki/natural-common-wealth-economic-rent-canada/)* (2023) cites the Foundation's economic-rent article for its working definition of rent and for the argument that rent recovery does not deter extraction (fns 21, 27), and adopts "the proposed royalty rate proposed by the Henry George Foundation of Canada" — **rent at 24% of the total value of mineral production**, yielding the paper's $11.3 billion/year mining-rent scenario (fn 32, Table 5).[5] [VERIFY: the Foundation's own primary document stating the 24% royalty proposal — the rate is confirmed as attributed in the Common Wealth Canada paper, but the underlying Foundation publication was not located this session.] The Foundation has in turn covered Common Wealth Canada's proposal on its news pages.[6]
