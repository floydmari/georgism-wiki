## Overview

The **Henry George School of Social Science** (HGSSS) was founded in New York City in **1932** by **Oscar H. Geiger**, a scholar who "saw the need for a wider popular understanding of political economy," and was chartered by the Board of Regents of the University of the State of New York.[1][2] It emerged in the aftermath of the Great Depression, as part of a reform movement seeking to advance the land value tax (or single tax) that George had argued for in *[Progress and Poverty](/wiki/progress-and-poverty/)*.[3] It provides **free** courses in political economy taught from a Georgist perspective, and has operated for more than ninety years, with branches and affiliates across the United States and internationally.

## History

From its New York base the School spread quickly: the first Chicago class was held in **1934**, taught by Henry L. T. Tideman, who also trained volunteer instructors; through the 1930s and 1940s classes ran in libraries, park field houses, and community halls.[2] The School's early decades were chronicled by its longtime director **Robert Clancy** in "The Henry George School's First Twenty-Five Years" (1957), which recorded the institution entering "its second quarter-century" as a vehicle for teaching George's philosophy.[4] The School maintains a historical archive of its records dating from 1932.[5]

## Role

The School's mission is educational: to make accessible the analysis of land, rent, and taxation that George set out in *Progress and Poverty*. It offers introductory and advanced courses, webinars, and public lectures, and has introduced generations of students and activists to Georgist economics. Free tuition reflects the movement's belief that economic understanding should not itself be gated.

## Significance

Alongside research bodies like the [Lincoln Institute](/wiki/lincoln-institute/) and the [Schalkenbach Foundation](/wiki/schalkenbach-foundation/), the Henry George School is the principal **educational** institution sustaining the Georgist tradition — and it was among the member bodies coordinated by the [Council of Georgist Organizations](/wiki/council-of-georgist-organizations/).
