## Overview

**BC Assessment** (legally the British Columbia Assessment Authority) is the
Crown corporation that produces the independent, province-wide property
valuations used as the basis for property taxation across
[British Columbia](/wiki/british-columbia/), Canada. It was created in **1974**
following a unanimous recommendation from a provincial all-party committee that
an independent assessment agency replace the previous system of separate
municipal assessments, under legislation (the Assessment Authority Act) giving it
the mandate to "establish and maintain assessments that are uniform in the whole
of British Columbia."[1][2] It produced the province's first independent,
impartial assessment roll within six months of its creation.[1] Today it values
on the order of **two million properties**, collectively worth over
**$1 trillion**, under a mandate — set out in the Assessment Act — to "accurately
value and classify all properties in British Columbia, producing assessments that
are fair, equitable and uniform."[2] It is governed by a Board of Directors
accountable to the responsible provincial minister.[2]

## Relevance to Land Value Taxation

Because BC Assessment already separates land value from improvement value on its
assessment rolls, [Common Wealth Canada](/wiki/common-wealth-canada/) has argued
that its existence gives British Columbia a head start most jurisdictions lack
for reviving a comprehensive [land value tax](/wiki/land-value-tax/) — the
administrative infrastructure a modern LVT would need already exists and is
maintained annually.[3] This case is developed on the
[British Columbia](/wiki/british-columbia/) place page, in the context of
Vancouver's historical land-only municipal tax era (1910–1984). Georgist assessor
[Ted Gwartney](/wiki/ted-gwartney/) worked within the BC assessment system
(described in earlier sources as the "British Columbia Assessment Authority")
during part of his career, experience he later drew on in his widely cited
practitioner's essay on land valuation.[4]

## Nuances and Limits

- **A neutral valuation body, not an advocate.** BC Assessment values property
  under BC's existing capital-value (land + improvements) property tax system; it
  takes no position on land value taxation. The LVT-readiness argument above is
  Common Wealth Canada's own advocacy case, not BC Assessment's.
- **Thin on independent institutional detail.** This page currently covers BC
  Assessment's founding, mandate, and scale from its own materials and secondary
  accounts; a fuller institutional history (governance evolution, funding model,
  relationship to municipal tax rates) is not yet compiled here.
