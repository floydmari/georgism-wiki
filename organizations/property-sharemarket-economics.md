## Overview

**Property Sharemarket Economics (PSE)**, styled Property Share Market Economics, is a commercial subscription investment-research service co-run by [Phillip J. Anderson](/wiki/phillip-j-anderson/) and [Akhil Patel](/wiki/akhil-patel/), built around the **[18-year land cycle](/wiki/18-year-land-cycle/)**.[1] It grew out of Anderson's Economic Indicator Services, founded in 1991, and today publishes newsletters, market commentary, and a proprietary "Property Cycle clock" for subscribers seeking to time real estate, equity, and commodity markets against the cycle.[1] The service's own marketing describes "almost five decades' experience in cycles analysis" and a track record of "calling all of the main turns in the markets and the economy," including naming the 2007 market top years in advance — claims made by the service itself rather than an independently audited record.[1]

PSE is the modern practitioner/forecasting arm of the Georgist land-cycle tradition this wiki traces from [Homer Hoyt](/wiki/homer-hoyt/) through [Fred Harrison](/wiki/fred-harrison/) to Anderson and Patel — the point where that lineage becomes a commercial product rather than a policy argument. Patel is the author of *[The Secret Wealth Advantage](/wiki/patel-secret-wealth-advantage/)* (2023) and Anderson of *[The Secret Life of Real Estate and Banking](/wiki/anderson-secret-life-real-estate/)* (2008); both books are, in effect, PSE's public-facing statements of the underlying model.[1][2]
