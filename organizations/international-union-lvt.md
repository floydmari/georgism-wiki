## Overview

The **International Union for Land Value Taxation** (the IU), founded in **1926**, is the worldwide federation of Georgist and land-value-taxation organisations. It links national groups — from the UK and Australia to the Americas and beyond — into a coordinated international movement.

## Role

The IU holds international conferences, publishes on land and tax policy, and represents the land-value-taxation movement in international forums (including consultative status at the United Nations). It serves as the connective tissue among the many national bodies — [Prosper Australia](/wiki/prosper-australia/), [Common Ground USA](/wiki/common-ground-usa/), the [Henry George Foundation](/wiki/schalkenbach-foundation/) tradition, and others — sustaining Georgism as a global rather than purely national project.

## Significance

The IU's near-century of continuous operation reflects the international reach Henry George achieved in his lifetime, when *[Progress and Poverty](/wiki/progress-and-poverty/)* was translated worldwide and inspired movements on several continents.
