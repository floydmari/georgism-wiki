## Overview

**UBI Works** is a Toronto-based Canadian nonprofit that campaigns for basic income, launched publicly in **August 2019** with the stated aim of telling "the economic and human stories behind basic income" and, in its current self-description, of making basic income "an election-winning issue."[1][2] It was founded by technology entrepreneur [Floyd Marinescu](/wiki/floyd-marinescu/) (CEO of C4Media), who — per his UBI Works bio — had earlier organized, with Tehama CEO Paul Vallée, an October 2018 letter of 120 Canadian CEOs endorsing basic income; the organization now claims over 180 CEO and business-leader signatories.[3][2] These membership and endorsement figures are the organization's own self-descriptions and have not been independently audited for this page.

UBI Works is an **advocacy organization**, and its economic claims (e.g., that a national guaranteed income could grow Canada's economy by $80 billion/year and create 600,000 jobs) are its own campaign framings of commissioned and third-party research, not independently adjudicated findings; per this wiki's source-quality hierarchy they should be read the same way as [Common Wealth Canada](/wiki/common-wealth-canada/)'s advocacy-stage estimates.[2]

## Relevance to Georgist Economics

Although UBI Works is a basic-income organization rather than a Georgist one, it distinguishes **guaranteed basic income** (poverty alleviation) from **"UBI dividends to share common wealth"** — the [citizen's dividend](/wiki/citizens-dividend/) framing — and it is the direct organizational predecessor of Canada's most active land-rent research group:[2]

- **Commissioned common-assets research.** In 2021 UBI Works issued a request for research proposals, "Calculating the Value of Common Assets and Associated Economic Rents in Canada," to quantify the value of Canada's common assets and their rents.[4] The eventual product of that agenda, *[Natural Common Wealth and Economic Rent in Canada](/wiki/natural-common-wealth-economic-rent-canada/)* (2023), was published by [Common Wealth Canada](/wiki/common-wealth-canada/) — an organization created by the same team, and the January 2023 working paper still directed feedback to a ubiworks.ca address.[5]
- **"Canada Land Value Time Series (1990–2022)".** The *Natural Common Wealth* paper's land-rent method rests on the average 8.32%/year growth in Canadian land values since 1990; its footnote 62 sources that calculation to a "UBI Works Analysis … Canada Land Value Time Series (1990-2022)," a spreadsheet analysis of Statistics Canada's National Balance Sheet land data.[5]
- **Team continuity.** UBI Works' team page lists Ben Earle (General Manager), Liam Wilkinson (Public Relations), Ken Yang (Media & Content), and Floyd Marinescu (Founder) — the same four people bylined as authors of the *Natural Common Wealth* paper.[3][5] Common Wealth Canada describes itself as founded by the UBI Works team to make the shared-wealth "pre-distribution" case separately from poverty-focused basic-income arguments.[6]
