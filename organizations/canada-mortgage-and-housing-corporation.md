## Overview

The Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC) is Canada's federal crown corporation and national housing agency, incorporated by act of the 19th Canadian Parliament in December 1945 and taking effect January 1, 1946, as the successor to the wartime Wartime Housing Corporation.[1] Known as Central Mortgage and Housing until 1979, its founding mandate was to house returning war veterans and administer the National Housing Act; today it administers federal housing programs, acts as Canada's primary mortgage insurer, and produces housing-market research and data used throughout Canadian housing policy.[1]

CMHC's research is one of this wiki's recurring sources on Canadian housing. Its September 2021 *Housing Market Assessment* rated Canada's national housing market "High" vulnerability — combining newly detected price acceleration with persistent overvaluation — and its own analysis in that edition explicitly found that quick-resale speculation was **not** the main driver of Montreal's price acceleration, a data point the wiki treats as a caution against over-attributing Canadian price dynamics to speculative flipping.[2] CMHC data and reporting also underlie the wiki's coverage of Canadian development-charge and land-value-capture debates and municipal vacancy taxation.[3][4] Together with independent academic decompositions, CMHC's institutional findings feed the wiki's broader case that Canadian housing unaffordability traces to land, not construction costs.[5]
