## Overview

**Shepheard-Walwyn (Publishers) Ltd** is an independent London-based publishing house, incorporated on 29 October 1971, that has operated for more than five decades with a list weighted toward "ethical economics."[1] Under publisher **Anthony Werner**, who took over the firm in 1979, Shepheard-Walwyn became the principal UK-based publisher of modern Georgist economics, issuing works by [Fred Harrison](/wiki/fred-harrison/) — including *[The Power in the Land](/wiki/harrison-power-in-the-land/)*, *[Boom Bust](/wiki/harrison-boom-bust/)*, and *[Ricardo's Law](/wiki/harrison-ricardos-law/)* — and by [Phillip J. Anderson](/wiki/phillip-j-anderson/), including *[The Secret Life of Real Estate and Banking](/wiki/anderson-secret-life-real-estate/)*, as well as reissued editions of [Henry George](/wiki/henry-george/)'s own books and Mason Gaffney and Fred Harrison's *[The Corruption of Economics](/wiki/gaffney-harrison-corruption-of-economics/)*.[2] In 2017, Werner and Harrison were jointly honored with Economic Justice awards (Advocate and Educator, respectively) for their contribution to the Georgist field.[3]

## Significance

As Schalkenbach is the historic US custodian of George's own texts, Shepheard-Walwyn functions as something close to a UK counterpart for the modern Georgist canon — the publisher most consistently willing to bring 18-year-land-cycle and land-value-tax economics to a general readership outside academic presses.
