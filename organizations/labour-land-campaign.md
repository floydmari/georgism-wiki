## Overview

The **Labour Land Campaign** advocates for [land value taxation](/wiki/land-value-tax/) within the British Labour movement and the wider UK left. It carries forward a tradition in British politics — running through the [1909 People's Budget](/wiki/1909-peoples-budget/) — that treats taxing the unearned value of land as a progressive cause.

## Work

The Campaign produces briefings and policy arguments aimed at trade unionists, Labour members, and policymakers, framing LVT as a tool against land monopoly, housing unaffordability, and inequality. It tracks land-tax developments in UK politics and presses for the idea within party policy debates.
