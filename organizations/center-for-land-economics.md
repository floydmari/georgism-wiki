## Overview

The **Center for Land Economics** is a contemporary nonprofit research organisation co-founded by [Lars Doucet](/wiki/lars-doucet/) and Greg Miller. It represents the modern, empirically minded wing of the Georgist revival, focusing on the practical questions that determine whether land value taxation can work in the real world.

## Focus

Its central concern is **land assessment** — building better methods and data to value land separately from improvements, directly tackling the most common [objection to LVT](/wiki/land-cannot-be-assessed/). It also produces research and tools to help jurisdictions evaluate and implement land-based taxation.

## Significance

Where older organisations preserve and teach the tradition, the Center for Land Economics aims to **operationalise** it — turning Georgist theory into deployable assessment technology and policy guidance for the 2020s.
