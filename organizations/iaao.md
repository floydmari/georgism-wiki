## Overview

The **International Association of Assessing Officers (IAAO)** is a nonprofit, educational and research association of government assessment officials, founded in 1934 in the United States as the National Association of Assessing Officers and renamed international in 1959 to reflect growing membership outside the US; it now has more than 8,500 members worldwide.[1] IAAO develops and publishes technical standards for property valuation and assessment administration, including its Standard on Mass Appraisal of Real Property and standards for ratio studies that test assessment accuracy and uniformity across a jurisdiction.[2]

Two of those standards — the [Standard on Ratio Studies (2013) and the Standard on Property Tax Policy (revised 2020)](/wiki/iaao-standards/) — were read in full for this wiki rather than cited only at second hand; the ratio-studies standard defines the COD/PRD/PRB accuracy metrics the wiki's assessment-quality claims rest on, while the policy standard, notably, says essentially nothing about land value or split-rate taxation as a design choice.[3]

## Relevance to Georgism

Because a [land value tax](/wiki/land-value-tax/) requires assessing land separately from buildings, IAAO's mass-appraisal standards are the professional infrastructure most often invoked to answer the objection that [land value can't be assessed accurately](/wiki/land-cannot-be-assessed/): the association formalizes exactly the [statistical and cooperative-game methods](/wiki/mass-appraisal-methods/) — hedonic regression, comparable-sales, ratio studies — that make routine land/building separation possible at scale.[2]
