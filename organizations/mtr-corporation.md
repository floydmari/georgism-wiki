## Overview

The **MTR Corporation** (MTRC) operates Hong Kong's metro system and is the world's most-cited example of financing public transit directly from the [land value capture](/wiki/land-value-capture/) it creates. Under its "**Rail plus Property**" (R+P) model, the Hong Kong government grants MTRC development rights over land at and above new stations and depots, for which MTRC pays a land premium based on the pre-railway value of that land. MTRC then partners with private developers to build housing and commercial space on the sites, and shares in the resulting development profits.[1] Because a new rail line raises the value of adjoining land, capturing part of that uplift lets the railway fund its own construction rather than relying on fares or government subsidy alone; property-related income has supplied more than half of MTRC's total revenue.[1]

The model depends on an institutional arrangement in which a single, partly government-owned corporation controls both the transit system and the development rights over the land its lines make more valuable — a combination few other transit agencies have.[1] Hong Kong's wider land system, in which nearly all land is government-owned and leased rather than sold freehold, is what makes granting MTRC these development rights administratively straightforward (see [Hong Kong](/wiki/hong-kong/)).
