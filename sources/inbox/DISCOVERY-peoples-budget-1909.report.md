## Events

- 29 April 1909 — Lloyd George presents the Budget to the Commons
- November 1909 — House of Lords rejects the Budget
- January 1910 — general election (Liberals returned, reduced majority)
- April 1910 — Budget eventually passed
- December 1910 — second general election
- 1911 — Parliament Act curtails Lords' veto power
- 1908 — old-age pensions introduced

## Objections

- Right-wing Liberals feared the Budget would promote class-based politics and destroy the Liberal party
- Unionists opposed direct taxation and preferred tariff reform
- Treasury orthodoxy resisted the boldness of the Budget
