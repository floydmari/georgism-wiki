## Concepts

- Economic rent · concepts · Returns from exclusive possession of scarce factor above production cost; central to classical and Georgist economics · Ch.1 §1.3, Ch.3 §3.2
- Land value tax (LVT) · concepts · Levy on unimproved land value; preferred by Smith, Mill, George; most efficient tax · Ch.1, Ch.3 §3.3, Ch.7 §7.3
- Single tax · concepts · Henry George's comprehensive land value tax to replace all other taxes · Ch.3 §3.3
- Marginal productivity theory · concepts · Neoclassical theory that incomes reflect contribution to production; conflates land with capital · Ch.3 §3.4, Ch.6 §6.3
- Neoclassical economics · concepts · School treating economy as self-equilibrating; conflated land with capital · Ch.1 Box 1.1, Ch.3 §3.4
- Classical political economy · concepts · School recognizing land as distinct factor of production; advocated land taxes · Ch.3 §3.2
- Financialisation · concepts · Transformation of land/housing into financial instruments; speculative asset vs use value · Ch.5 Box 5.2
- House price-credit feedback cycle · concepts · Self-reinforcing cycle between mortgage credit, house prices, and land values · Ch.5 §5.2
- Land-credit cycle / financial cycle · concepts · Longer-term (~16–18 year) cycle driven by land/property values, distinct from business cycle · Ch.5 §5.6
- Collateral channel · concepts · Land/property as preferred bank collateral; enables additional borrowing and consumption · Ch.5 §5.3, §5.5
- Home equity withdrawal (HEW) · concepts · Borrowing against rising property values for consumption; 'privatised Keynesianism' · Ch.5 §5.5
- Lifecycle model / permanent income hypothesis · concepts · Mainstream theory of consumption smoothing; neglects credit constraints and land dynamics · Ch.5 §5.3
- Balance sheet recession · concepts · When all sectors deleverage simultaneously; deeper and longer than normal recessions · Ch.5 §5.6
- Securitisation · concepts · Pooling and repackaging mortgages into tradable securities; RMBS · Ch.5 Box 5.5
- Residential capitalism · concepts · System where homeownership, credit, and consumption are intertwined · Ch.4 §4.5
- Property-owning democracy · concepts · Political goal of spreading homeownership; Conservative strategy · Ch.4 §4.3
- NIMBYism · concepts · Homeowners resisting development to protect asset values · Ch.2 §2.4
- Rent-seeking · concepts · Directing effort toward getting larger share of pie rather than increasing it · Ch.1 §1.3
- Primitive Accumulation · concepts · Marx's concept of forced removal of peasants from land · Ch.2 §2.2
- Lockean proviso · concepts · Property natural only while sufficient common land exists · Ch.2 §2.2
- Eminent domain · concepts · State power to expropriate property with compensation · Ch.2 §2.2
- Compulsory purchase · concepts · UK equivalent of eminent domain; key tool for land value capture · Ch.2 §2.6, Ch.7 §7.2
- Betterment / planning gain · concepts · Uplift in land value from grant of planning permission · Ch.4 §4.3, Ch.7 §7.6
- Residual land valuation · concepts · Method developers use to price land: sales value minus costs · Ch.4 Box 4.7
- Speculative house builder model · concepts · Cyclical build-finance-sell process; dominant UK model · Ch.4 Box 4.6

- Sovereign money / narrow banking · concepts · Only central bank creates money; banks as true intermediaries · Ch.7 §7.4

## Objections

- "Land and capital are interchangeable" (neoclassical conflation) · objections · Clark/Wicksteed argued factors are substitutable; book argues fundamental differences · Ch.3 §3.4–§3.5
- "Property is theft" (Proudhon) · objections · Anarchist objection to private land ownership · Ch.2 §2.5
- "Property is liberty" (Jefferson/Proudhon) · objections · Counter-argument: ownership enables freedom and development · Ch.2 §2.5
- "Just build more homes" (supply-side solution) · objections · Mainstream policy response; book argues insufficient without addressing credit/land dynamics · Ch.3 §3.8, Ch.4
- "Low interest rates cause high house prices" (universal explanation) · objections · Book shows country-specific institutional factors matter more · Ch.5 §5.2
- "Marginal productivity determines income distribution" · objections · Neoclassical theory; book shows it fails to account for land rents · Ch.6 §6.3
- "Banks are financial intermediaries" · objections · Mainstream textbook view; book argues banks create money · Ch.5 §5.1, Ch.7 §7.7
- "Mortgage credit is welfare-enhancing" (lifecycle model) · objections · Standard theory; book shows it creates unsustainable debt and fragility · Ch.5 §5.3
- "George's single tax was too narrow" (socialist critique) · objections · Socialists rejected focus on land tax alone; led to George's isolation · Ch.3 §3.7
- "Rent controls create shortages" · objections · Common economist objection to rent control · Ch.4 §4.3
- "LVT is impractical to implement" · objections · Political and practical barriers; valuation challenges; income-poor pensioner problem · Ch.7 §7.3
- "Property tax is too salient/unpopular" · objections · Visibility makes property taxes politically difficult · Ch.7 §7.3
- "Free markets will clear land prices" · objections · Neoclassical assumption; book argues land's fixed supply prevents this · Ch.1 §1.3, Ch.7 §7.1
- "Piketty's r > g explains inequality" · objections · Book argues rising land values, not returns to capital, drive wealth-to-income ratio · Ch.6 §6.4
- "Globalisation/superstar effect explains inequality" · objections · Traditional explanations that neglect land's role · Ch.6 §6.3
- "Taxing land will crash property values and destabilise banks" · objections · Key practical objection to LVT; book proposes phased introduction · Ch.7 §7.3
