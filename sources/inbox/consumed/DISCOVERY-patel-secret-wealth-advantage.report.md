## Notes

1. This book is primarily an investment guide, not a Georgist policy treatise, but it is deeply rooted in Georgist economics. The entire analytical framework derives from Henry George's law of economic rent and the land-cycle research lineage: George → Hoyt → Harrison → Anderson → Patel. The book explicitly endorses George's remedy (land value tax) as the only solution that would eliminate the cycle (Ch. 5, Ch. 9), while noting it has been deliberately suppressed.

2. The book's unique contribution to the wiki is its detailed stage-by-stage description of the 18-year cycle (Start → Expansion → Peak → Recession → Land Boom → Mania → Summit → Crash → Rescue), its integration of the Kondratiev Long Cycle, and its treatment of bank money creation and MMT/monetary sovereignty as cycle amplifiers. The "Handbook of Wealth Secrets" investment guidance at each stage is novel content.

3. Several people mentioned are primarily historical figures used as illustrative examples (e.g., J.P. Morgan, Henry Ford, Steve Jobs, Mark Twain) rather than Georgist intellectuals. They may not warrant full people pages unless the wiki decides to track the "new technology at cycle Start" pattern.

4. The book references its own website (thesecretwealthadvantage.com) frequently for supplementary resources. These are not included as candidates as they are not independently citable.

5. The Appendix 1 ("Dates of Real Estate Cycles") provides a comprehensive table of cycle highs and troughs for multiple countries (US, UK, Japan, Germany, France, Australia, etc.) from 1800 to 2026. This is a valuable data source that could feed a wiki page on cycle chronology.

6. The book's treatment of Elizabeth Magie and the Landlord's Game (Ch. 5) is the most detailed account in any wiki source book and directly connects Monopoly's origins to Georgist education and the corruption of economics.

7. The book discusses Russia's post-Soviet transition extensively (Ch. 5), including the Gorbachev letter signed by 30 economists (3 Nobel laureates), the oligarchy formation, and Putin's rise — all framed as consequences of failing to implement land value tax. This is a significant geopolitical narrative not covered elsewhere in the wiki.
