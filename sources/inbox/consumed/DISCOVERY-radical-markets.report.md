## Objections

1. ❌ COST will upend daily life / forced sales objection — objections · "Coming home to find apartment no longer yours"; addressed with low turnover-rate taxes, grace periods, clustering · Ch.1 §"Optimizing Public Leases"
2. ❌ COST discourages investment (investment inefficiency) — objections · George's tax expropriates all land value; COST solved by moderate rate; quadratic structure · Ch.1 §"Name Your Price"
3. ❌ COST is like slavery / coercive (human capital extension) — objections · COST on human capital could feel like slavery; authors argue current system is also coercive · Conclusion §"Economy"
4. ❌ Georgism can't separate land value from improvements — objections · Empire State Building example; land + structure intertwined · Ch.1 §"Markets without Property"
5. ❌ COST enables predatory taking / sabotage — objections · Owners could sabotage to avoid forced sale; addressed by pricing above reservation value · Ch.1 note 43
6. ❌ QV allows wealthy to buy influence — objections · Voice credits vs. money; monetized QV addressed; COST equalizes first · Ch.2, Conclusion
7. ❌ QV vulnerable to collusion / vote-buying / fraud — objections · Requires legal enforcement and social norms; same as 1p1v · Ch.2 §"Quadratic Voting"
8. ❌ QV only works if voters are rational/selfish — objections · Non-outcome motivations may distort; requires civic norms · Ch.2
9. ❌ Open borders / unlimited immigration will suppress native wages — objections · Stolper-Samuelson; VIP shares gains with natives · Ch.3
10. ❌ Immigration is exploitative / like indentured servitude — objections · VIP compared to H1-B and J-1; workers free to leave · Ch.3 §"Democratizing Visas"
11. ❌ Immigration increases visible inequality (subordinate migrant class) — objections · Temporary/circular migration; reduces global inequality; already have subordinate class (undocumented) · Ch.3 §"Could VIP Work?"
12. ❌ Institutional investor limits reduce diversification — objections · 50-stock portfolios achieve 90% diversification; cross-industry diversification preserved; index fund exception · Ch.4 §"Diversification"
13. ❌ Data payment would undermine intrinsic motivation (Tom Sawyer problem) — objections · Bénabou-Tirole; paying signals labor, may reduce entertainment value · Ch.5 §"Digital Whitewash"
14. ❌ Central planning could replace markets with computers — objections · Shalizi: computational impossibility; Moore's Law may eventually bridge gap · Epilogue
15. ❌ Markets undermine moral economies / community — objections · Sandel, Bowles; markets destroy solidarity; authors counter: Radical Markets build trust · Intro, Conclusion
16. ❌ Siren servers won't voluntarily pay for data — objections · Monopsony power, network effects, breaking the "spell"; requires regulation/union pressure · Ch.5 §"Digital Whitewash"
17. ❌ COST/antitrust proposals face overwhelming lobbying backlash — objections · Top 10% would lose monopoly rents; political feasibility challenge · Ch.4 §"Law on Our Side"
