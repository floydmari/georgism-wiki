## Objections

- "English did not recognize Indian property rights" — consensus view Banner argues against, pp.10–11
- "Indians couldn't understand what settlers meant by a sale" — Banner challenges, p.2
- "Transactions were purely involuntary / conquest" — Banner challenges, p.3
- "Transactions were purely voluntary / fair purchase" — Banner also challenges, p.3
- "Johnson v. M'Intosh" correctly reflects colonial practice — Banner argues it misrepresents, p.12
- Vacuum domicilium / terra nullius as basis for dispossession — Banner argues not dominant in practice, pp.10–11
