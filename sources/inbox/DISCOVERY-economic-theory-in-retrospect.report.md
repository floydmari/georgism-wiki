## Objections
- Marshallian objection to single tax · objections · All agents earn rents in short run; even differential rents are incentive payments in long run · Ch.3, §11 (lines 4374-4377)
- Difficulty distinguishing site value from betterment · objections · George failed to clarify distinction between tax on site values vs. tax on improvements · Ch.3, §11 (lines 4386-4388)
- Progress and Poverty was "thirty years out of date" · objections · Blaug argues George's economics was outdated classical analysis at publication · Ch.3, §11 (line 4414)
- Land speculation performs an economic function · objections · Speculators bear risk of mistaken forecasts; nationalisation shifts risk to community · Ch.3, §11 (lines 4406-4411)
- Site value taxation raises limited revenue · objections · Only significant revenue in rapidly growing cities · Ch.3, §11 (line 4395)
- Property rights of landowners vs. stimulus to improvement · objections · Constitutional issue of confiscating established property rights · Ch.3, §11 (lines 4398-4400)
- Walras's land nationalisation compensation flaw · objections · Full compensation via bonds would consume all rental receipts in interest payments · Ch.3, §11 (lines 4427-4438)
- Böhm-Bawerk's critique of Marx's surplus value · objections · Interest not specific to capitalism; time preference general · Ch.6, §10
- Wicksteed's product exhaustion theorem critique · objections · Barone accused Wicksteed of plagiarism; constant returns assumption problematic · Ch.11
- Cambridge reswitching critique · objections · Samuelson conceded that capital reswitching undermines aggregate capital · Ch.12, §15-18
