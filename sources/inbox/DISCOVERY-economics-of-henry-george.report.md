## Objections

- George lacked formal training — academic economists' objection, p.3
- George's methodology was "an empty set" — contemporaries' view, p.5
- George "failed to understand Marshall and Böhm-Bawerk" — Schumpeter, p.5
- George's rejection of marginal utility — seen as wrong by modern standards
- George's "single tax" panacea — dismissed by academic economists
- George as "perpetual outcast of the academic establishment" — p.20
- Professors "could clearly never accept as their teacher one 'whose education was of the mere common-school branches'" — p.20
- "Herd instinct" of academic economists — George's critique, p.20
