## Concepts

- Ricardo's Law · concepts · Spatial distribution of rent; declining productivity from centre to periphery; organising principle of economy · Ch 2, throughout
- Economic Rent · concepts · Surplus after wages and capital costs; price of civilisation; 'public value of land' (Marshall) · Ch 2.1
- Tax Clawback Scam · concepts · Rich claw back tax payments as capital gains in land values; poor subsidise rich · Ch 1, title concept
- ATCOR (All Taxes Come Out of Rent) · concepts · Implicit throughout: all public spending creates value captured as land rent; failure to collect it forces destructive taxes · Ch 1, Ch 4.2
- Deadweight Loss of Taxation · concepts · 30p loss per £1 raised by 'bad' taxes; £138bn UK loss in 2005/06; £1 trillion over 9 years of New Labour · Ch 3.4, Ch 5.2
- Margin of Production · concepts · Location where wages/interest just cover costs; no surplus/rent; sets wage benchmarks for economy · Ch 2.2, Ch 3
- Sub-marginal Zone · concepts · Area below economic margin; people driven there by taxation; 'No-Man's Land'; political creation · Ch 3.4, Ch 11
- Rent-Seeking · concepts · Manipulating laws to appropriate public value; not personal corruption but systematic rules · Indictment I
- Free Rider Problem · concepts · Landowners who receive public services without paying; taxes on poor fund their capital gains · Ch 4.2
- First Law of Social Dynamics · concepts · Harrison's principle: efficiency requires equalisation of rent across community; competition + cooperation · Ch 4.3, Ch 15
- Spatial Redistribution of Income · concepts · Tax-funded value flows from periphery to centre; captured by landowners; not random · Ch 1.4
- Agglomeration · concepts · Theory that clustering delivers productivity; Harrison argues it's used to justify London bias · Ch 5.2
- Synergistic City · concepts · Gaffney's alternative to agglomeration; transport investment surplus captured by land · Ch 5.3
- Integrated Pricing System · concepts · Unifying public and private prices; land rent as public revenue replacing taxes on wages/savings · Ch 14, Ch 15
- Consumer's Surplus · concepts · Value consumers willing to pay above market price; externalised into land rent · Ch 14.1, Box 14:1
- Property Cycle / Boom-Bust · concepts · Speculation in property drives economic cycles; LVT would smooth by removing windfall incentive · Ch 15.3

## Objections

- 'Market failure' doctrine · objections · Used to justify government intervention; actually masks governance failure; deflects from land market rules · Ch 14.2
- Hayek's conflation of land with capital · objections · Erased unique characteristics of rent as public value; led free marketers astray · Ch 14.3
- Hayek's rejection of Henry George's Single Tax · objections · Claimed it required state ownership of land (false); claimed practical difficulties (nonexistent); deterred free market advocates · Ch 14.4
- Fukuyama's denial of science of governance · objections · Asserted 'no optimal form of organisation'; legitimised art over science in public administration · Ch 4.3
- Malthusian blame-the-poor doctrine · objections · Attributed poverty to personal failings; continues in Blair government's health policy · Ch 3.2, Ch 4.1
- Marxist class conflict theory · objections · Inadequate explanation; ignores spatial dimension; state is villain not capitalist · Ch 1.4
- Progressive taxation as fair · objections · Direct taxes appear progressive but indirect taxes make total burden regressive; clawback via land market reverses it · Ch 1
