## Book Overview

*Boom Bust* is Fred Harrison's major theoretical and empirical work articulating the **18-year land cycle** — the thesis that capitalism's boom-bust rhythm is driven by speculation in land (economic rent), not by monetary policy, inflation, wars, or random shocks. Harrison predicted the 2008 financial crisis and the "Depression of 2010" as early as 1997 (in *The Chaos Makers*), and in this book (first published 2005) he provides the full theoretical framework, 400 years of historical evidence, and the proposed remedy: **land value taxation / rent-as-public-revenue**.

The book is organised in five parts:
- **Part I (Ch 1–4):** As Safe as Houses? — Diagnoses the Brown and Greenspan failures in the UK and US.
- **Part II (Ch 5–8):** Genesis of the Boom-Bust Cycle — The 18-year cycle, historical patterns (1700–2010), land speculation mechanics, Keynes's misdiagnosis.
- **Part III (Ch 9–11):** Anatomy of the First Global Cycle — The New Economy myth, the US launch, Gordon Brown's failures.
- **Part IV (Ch 12–14):** The Automatic Stabiliser — Counter-cyclical fiscal policy, Australia's land tax experience, dividends from democratised finance.
- **Part V (Ch 15–16):** The Reckoning — 2007 peak to downwave, 2010 depression economics.

## Key Thematic Notes

### The 18-Year Cycle Mechanism
- **14-year growth phase** determined by 5% compound interest on mortgages (money doubles in ~14 years at 5%)
- Divided into: **7-year recovery** → mid-cycle recession → **5-year acceleration** → **2-year Winner's Curse**
- **4-year recession phase** (Keynes's estimate: 3–5 years, avg 4)
- Origin: 5% was the usury law ceiling (1714); matched adult working life expectancy (~17.5 years); 20 years' purchase of rent = 5% yield
- First building society (Birmingham 1775) used 5% → 14-year terminating cycle

### The Prediction Record
- 1983 (*Power in the Land*): predicted 1992 recession ✓
- 1988: submitted warning memoranda to UK Treasury (ignored)
- 1996: warned Tony Blair's shadow cabinet (ignored by Brown)
- 1997 (*Chaos Makers*): predicted 2008 peak, 2010 depression ✓
- 2004: warned Brown's last chance was the 2004 budget (failed to act)

### The Remedy
- **Land value taxation**: annual charge on land site values, replacing income/consumption taxes
- **Not** a development land tax (tried and failed post-1947)
- Must be uniform rate on all land at current market values
- Counter-cyclical: removes capital gains incentive that drives speculation
- Revenue sufficient: Australian data shows land income ≈ total tax revenue
- G7 deadweight losses from bad taxes: ~$7 trillion/year (Tideman/Plassmann)
- UK potential gain: £880bn additional output (£15,000/person)

### The Political Failure
- 1909: Lloyd George's People's Budget (land taxes) → blocked by House of Lords
- 1930: Snowden's land valuation → killed by Neville Chamberlain 1932–34
- 1952: Australia abolished federal land tax → boom-bust resumed
- 1997–2007: Brown had 10 years and ignored all warnings
- G20 (2009): "strong, sustainable and balanced growth" × 21 in communiqué, zero structural reform
- Harrison predicts next cycle: bubble ~2019, crash ~2028
