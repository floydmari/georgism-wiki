**Mined (this wave's one pick): E4, "Tax Reform to Release Land"** (RFF
Forum, April 1972; published in Marion Clawson, ed., *Modernizing Urban Land
Policy*, 1973, pp. 115–151) — see
[research/gaffney-tax-reform-release-land](/wiki/gaffney-tax-reform-release-land/).
A 39-page treatise recasting local governments as "collective landowners"
practicing fiscal Mercantilism (tax-base dilution, tax export, amenity-
fencing, anti-competition zoning), with a technical appendix deriving three
distinct formal models: a bidding-power formula for why building taxes
systematically favor low-intensity uses (gas stations) over high-intensity
ones (apartments/high-rises) for the same site; a land-ripening-date
criterion showing building taxes defer development while land taxes
accelerate it through a wealth/cash-drain channel rather than the standard
neutral-incentive argument; and a differential-capitalization model of why
appreciating land gravitates to credit-advantaged "strong hands." Chosen
over strong competing candidates (see below) because it fills a genuine,
clean theoretical gap: the "collective landowner" framing predates, and is
far richer than, the wiki's existing 1976 Gaffney citation on
[concepts/nimbyism](/wiki/nimbyism/) (a priority correction, now recorded on
that page), and its formal models supply the first closed-form derivations
in the wiki's Gaffney corpus of mechanisms elsewhere stated only narratively
(the "strong hands" credit-concentration argument on
[concepts/land-monopoly](/wiki/land-monopoly/), now up to three independent
formal statements across 1961/1972-73/1973) or asserted without derivation
(the ripening-deferral argument on
[benefits/lvt-dampens-land-speculation](/wiki/lvt-dampens-land-speculation/)).
Registry: one new row (tier `important`, Scan Depth Heavy, native text, no
OCR needed). Text mirrored to `sources/gaffney/text/E4-TaxReformtoReleaseLand.CV.txt`.
