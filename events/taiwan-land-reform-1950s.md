Taiwan's land reform program of 1949–1953 implemented the "land-to-the-tiller" reforms, redistributing agricultural land and reducing tenancy, followed by the 1954 Statute for the Equalization of Land Rights which institutionalized Sun Yat-sen's land-value taxation principles. These reforms established the policy framework for Taiwan's subsequent land value capture mechanisms, including the land value increment tax. The Lincoln Institute case study documents this history as foundational context for understanding Taiwan's modern land policy.
