## Overview

Beginning in 2023, Detroit Mayor Mike Duggan championed a **"Land Value Tax Plan"** — a [split-rate](/wiki/split-rate-taxation/) reform that would sharply raise the tax rate on **land** while cutting the rate on **buildings (structures)**. It is the most prominent recent attempt to bring land value taxation to a major American city.

## The Rationale

Detroit suffers from extensive vacant land, abandoned lots, and speculative holding — much of it owned by investors awaiting appreciation. The plan aimed to **cut property taxes for most homeowners** while raising the burden on owners of vacant lots, surface parking, and blighted land, directly targeting [speculative vacancy](/wiki/speculative-vacancy/). Proponents argued it would reward building and use while penalising idle land-banking.

## Status

**The plan never reached the ballot and its enabling legislation died in the Michigan House.** The proposal required state authorisation to let Detroit split its tax rate. In September 2023 that authorisation was introduced as a five-bill package — **House Bills 4966–4970 of 2023, the "Land Tax Equity Act"** (lead sponsor Rep. Stephanie A. Young, tie-barred together). HB 4966 was reported from the House Tax Policy Committee, read a third time and amended on **11 October 2023 — and then "postponed for the day."** It never received a final House floor vote. House Speaker Joe Tate tabled the package after failing to secure the votes, and the Legislature broke for the rest of 2023 without passing it.

The bills were **never revived in 2024**. They stayed in what Crain's called "legislative limbo," and the **November 2024 referendum the plan was aimed at never happened**. With the 2023–2024 legislative session closing without passage, the package expired unenacted.

By Duggan's own post-mortem (December 2024), bipartisan support collapsed once the bills were on the floor: he said Republicans turned against the plan once they realised it was Speaker Tate's priority, while some progressive Democrats withheld their votes to trade for other priorities. Duggan did **not** seek re-election as mayor; he announced in December 2024 that he would run for **Michigan governor as an independent in 2026**. **Mary Sheffield won the November 2025 Detroit mayoral election** (becoming the city's first female mayor) and took office in January 2026; during her campaign she favoured a downtown **entertainment tax** to fund homeowner relief rather than pursuing the LVT.

As of mid-2026 the plan is dormant. In **November 2025 Duggan pledged to revive the land-value-tax effort if elected governor** — so its future is tied to the outcome of the **November 2026 governor's race, which is unresolved** at the time of writing. Regardless of its fate, the episode put land value taxation onto the agenda of a major US city and the national press.
