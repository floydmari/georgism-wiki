## Overview

On 7 November 1990, thirty Western economists published **"Socialise the Rent: An
Open Letter to Mikhail Gorbachev,"** urging the reforming Soviet Union to retain
land in public ownership and raise government revenue by charging rent for its
use — warning against repeating the West's enclosure-era mistake of giving land
rent away to private owners.[1] Signatories included Nobel laureates **Franco
Modigliani, James Tobin, Robert Solow, and [William Vickrey](/wiki/william-vickrey/)**,
plus William Baumol, [Mason Gaffney](/wiki/mason-gaffney/), Lowell Harriss, and
others; [Nicolaus Tideman](/wiki/nicolaus-tideman/) organized the signatures.[1][2]

The advice was not taken: Russia's 1990s privatizations gave away resource and
land rents — the outcome
[Gaffney and Harrison's *Corruption of Economics*](/wiki/gaffney-harrison-corruption-of-economics/)
later contrasted with China's lease-based path, and a key exhibit in the
[land-rent-as-revenue debate](/wiki/land-rent-could-fund-government/) about roads
not taken.
