From the Georgism Wiki

  • Resource Rents — Essay's core argument is that water's scarcity rent should be captured and shared.
  • Ecological Georgism — Extends Georgist rent-capture to natural resources and environmental externalities like water.
  • Economic Rent — Defines the surplus from scarcity that the essay identifies in market-priced water.
  • Pigouvian Taxation — Frames the social cost of water depletion as an unpriced taking from the commons.
  • Citizen's Dividend — The essay proposes sharing resource rent equally as cash dividends to residents.
