From the Georgism Wiki

  • Congestion Pricing — Direct application of marginal-cost pricing to congested roads and transit.
  • Henry George Theorem — Formalizes how public services are funded exactly by the land rent they create.
  • Public goods can be funded from the land rent they create — Empirical foundation for funding public goods from generated land rent.
  • Hotelling (1938): The General Welfare and Marginal-Cost Pricing — The classic paper arguing deficits from marginal-cost pricing be covered by land rent.
  • Land Value Capture — Broad family of tools recovering public-service-driven land value increases.
