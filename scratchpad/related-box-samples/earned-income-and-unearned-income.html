From the Georgism Wiki

  • Economic Rent — Defines the surplus from scarcity or privilege that underpins the essay's concept of unearned income.
  • Unearned Increment — Traces the moral and theoretical history of the unearned land-value gains central to the essay's distinction.
  • Transfer Earnings — Provides the modern textbook generalization of rent as earnings exceeding transfer earnings, deepening the earned/unearned split.
  • Quasi-Rent — Challenges the essay's clean dichotomy by introducing returns that look like rent but reward sunk investment.
  • Rent-Seeking — Extends the unearned income concept beyond land rent to wealth captured via economic or political power.
