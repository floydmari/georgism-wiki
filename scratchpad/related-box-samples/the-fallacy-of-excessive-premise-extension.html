From the Georgism Wiki

  • Land as Commons — The moral tradition the essay argues: land as common property by natural right, from Spencer through George.
  • Geolibertarianism — Directly fuses self-ownership with community ownership of land rent — the essay's core argument.
  • John Locke — Locke's labor-theory of property and 'enough and as good' proviso are central to the essay's reasoning.
  • Citizen's Dividend — The essay's proposal to distribute land rent directly to community members, exemplified by Alaska's fund.
  • Physiocracy and the Impôt Unique — The 18th-century single-tax-on-land-rent forerunner the essay explicitly cites as George's ancestry.
