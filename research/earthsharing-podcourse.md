## Summary

The **Georgist Podcourse** is a structured, 12-part audio course introducing the principles of Georgist economics, produced by [Earthsharing Australia](/wiki/earthsharing-australia/) (with [Karl Fitzgerald](/wiki/karl-fitzgerald/)) and broadcast on community radio.

## Origin and Format

The Podcourse was announced by Karl Fitzgerald on the Earthsharing Australia blog on **3 May 2019** as a format change of his long-running [*Renegade Economists*](/wiki/renegade-economists-podcast/) radio show: after "11 years of the weekly show," the program moved to a monthly slot — "the last Wednesday of the month from 6 – 6.30pm" on Melbourne community station **3CR** — with the podcast posted online a few days later and show notes hosted on parent body **Prosper Australia**'s website under the Renegade Economists tag.[1] The announcement describes the new format as a move "away from current affairs and onto a 12 part course" on Georgist economics — in Fitzgerald's words, "it's time to look at the founding principles to an earth rights democracy."[1] The first two installments listed are *Episode 1 — Property Rights tilt the playing field for generations* and *Episode 2 — Justice before Liberty, ok!*[1]

## Why It Matters

Unlike a conversational podcast, the Podcourse is **pedagogical** — a sequenced curriculum that builds understanding of land, rent, and taxation step by step. It is a useful free resource for someone who wants a guided introduction rather than scattered episodes, complementing the [Henry George School's](/wiki/henry-george-school/) free courses. It also marks the point where *Renegade Economists* — one of the movement's longest-running audio programs — shifted from weekly commentary to structured education.
