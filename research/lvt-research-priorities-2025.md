## Summary

This piece from the Progress and Poverty Institute lays out the **research agenda** for land value taxation going forward — a useful map of the field's open questions.

## Why It Matters

It identifies where the evidence is strong (e.g. land's [share of wealth](/wiki/capital-share-rise-is-land/), [incidence](/wiki/landlords-cannot-pass-lvt-to-tenants/)) and where more work is needed ([assessment](/wiki/land-cannot-be-assessed/) methods, affordability effects, transition design, macro modelling). For a wiki organised around an [Outcomes ⇄ Research](/wiki/) evidence graph, this is effectively a to-do list of the outcomes still to be firmly established — and a guide to where the next generation of studies (like the IMF and CEPR work catalogued here) is heading.
