## Summary

*Renegade Economists* was a radio show and podcast produced through
[Prosper Australia](/wiki/prosper-australia/) and hosted by
[Karl Fitzgerald](/wiki/karl-fitzgerald/). It ran from **September 2007 to November 2021** on
Melbourne community station **3CR**, broadcast on Wednesday evenings, and reached some **600
numbered episodes** by its final show.[1][2] Prosper describes its organising question directly:
if "'location, location, location' is such a core element to real estate investment strategy,
why was it all but ignored in economic theory?"[1]

## Coverage

Across its run the show covered [land taxation](/wiki/land-value-tax/), banking and monopoly
power, housing, [speculative vacancy](/wiki/speculative-vacancy/), and the
[18-year land cycle](/wiki/18-year-land-cycle/), typically with an expert guest. It also produced
a seven-part Georgist "podcourse" built around Adam Smith's canons of taxation and the
beneficiary-pays principle (distinct from Earthsharing's longer
[podcourse](/wiki/earthsharing-podcourse/)).[1] The final broadcast, Show 601 ("On A Win,"
24 November 2021), marked the passage of **Victoria's Windfall Gains Tax** — a levy on the
uplift in land value from rezoning — and closed the series.[2][3] Earlier episodes were
transcribed on the Earthsharing website, and the later back-catalogue is archived on Mixcloud.[1]

## Why It Matters

The show is one of the largest sustained bodies of Georgist audio anywhere, and a significant
archive for following contemporary land-economics debate from the Australian movement that has
kept land value taxation politically live — the same movement whose advocacy accompanied
Victoria's rezoning windfall levy. It is carried here as a practitioner/advocacy navigation
source: the empirical claims aired on it are cited on their own pages, not through the podcast.
