## Summary

This 2011 NBER paper (w17035) by Casey Rothschild and Florian Scheuer extends optimal-tax theory to a world where some high incomes come from **rent-seeking** rather than productive contribution.

## Key Finding

When part of top earnings reflects rent extraction (capturing existing value) rather than new production, the optimal income tax differs from the standard model: higher marginal rates can be efficient because they discourage socially wasteful rent-seeking without sacrificing genuine production. The paper provides a rigorous foundation for treating [rent-seeking](/wiki/rent-seeking/) as central to tax design — complementing the Georgist case for taxing rents directly at their source.
