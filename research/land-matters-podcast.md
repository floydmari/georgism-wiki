## Summary

*Land Matters* is the podcast of the [Lincoln Institute of Land Policy](/wiki/lincoln-institute/),
hosted by the Institute's senior fellow **Anthony Flint** — a career journalist, contributing
editor of the Institute's *Land Lines* magazine, and a correspondent for Bloomberg CityLab and
*The Boston Globe*.[2] Billed as "a behind the scenes look at what makes cities tick," the show is
released in seasons and covers land use, property and [land taxation](/wiki/land-value-tax/),
housing affordability, climate resilience, and municipal finance through interviews with
researchers and practitioners.[1]

## Coverage

The Institute frames the podcast around the idea that "a big part of innovative solutions can be
traced back to land," whether the subject is "financing infrastructure, adapting to climate
change, or building more affordable housing."[1] Representative episodes span the Institute's
research agenda — for example "Zoning and Its Discontents," "Talking Tech for Cities," "Building
Vibrant Communities," and "Leader in a Land of Extremes" — touching land-based public finance,
[value capture](/wiki/land-value-capture/), and the regeneration of post-industrial cities.[1]

## Why It Matters

As the audio companion to the field's premier research institution, *Land Matters* offers a more
**policy- and evidence-oriented** counterpart to advocacy podcasts — useful for the empirical,
institutional side of land-value questions (assessment, value capture, municipal finance). It
reflects the Lincoln Institute's measured, research-first approach, and functions here as a
navigation source: the underlying studies its episodes draw on are cited on their own pages, not
through the podcast.
