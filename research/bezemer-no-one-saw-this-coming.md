## Summary

Dirk Bezemer (University of Groningen) assembled the documented public record of
who anticipated the 2008 credit crisis *in advance, with reasoning* — requiring a
public prediction, an account of the mechanism, and a timeline. His roughly a
dozen qualifiers (including [Fred Harrison](/wiki/fred-harrison/) and
[Fred Foldvary](/wiki/fred-foldvary/)) shared a common feature: **accounting /
flow-of-funds models** tracking credit and asset markets, against the equilibrium
models that dominated policy.[1] MPRA Paper 15892 (June 2009); the analysis also
circulated as a University of Groningen working paper and in a VoxEU column.[1][2]

This page de-references the citation carried by
[the cycle narrative](/wiki/land-speculation-causes-cycles/) and
[the 18.6-year-cycle research page](/wiki/progress-18-6-year-cycle/): Bezemer is
the *independent, non-Georgist* auditor of the prediction record — which is exactly
why the wiki cites him rather than the predictors' own retrospectives. Stub pending
