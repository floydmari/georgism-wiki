## Summary

This 2021 *European Economic Review* paper by Bonnet, Chapelle, Trannoy, and Wasmer is the leading European confirmation of the land-centred view of modern wealth. Its title doubles as a thesis: land is back as the dominant component of wealth; it should be taxed on efficiency and equity grounds; and it can be taxed in practice.

## Key Findings

1. **Land drives the wealth boom.** Using French and other European data, the authors show the long-run rise in wealth-to-income ratios is overwhelmingly a rise in **land values**, not produced capital — independently confirming [Rognlie (2015)](/wiki/rognlie-capital-share/) on a different dataset.
2. **A land tax dominates a capital tax.** They demonstrate that shifting taxation from capital to land raises welfare, because taxing land has no efficiency cost while taxing capital discourages investment.
