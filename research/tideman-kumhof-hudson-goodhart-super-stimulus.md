## Summary

This CEPR discussion paper (2021) by **[Nicolaus Tideman](/wiki/nicolaus-tideman/)**,
**Michael Kumhof** (ex-IMF, Bank of England), **[Michael Hudson](/wiki/michael-hudson/)**,
and **Charles Goodhart** (LSE) models a revenue-neutral shift of taxation onto land
rent and finds large output gains — a "balanced-budget super-stimulus" — from
removing distortionary taxes on labor and capital while taxing the fixed factor.[1]
Its weight is the author roster: a mainstream monetary economist of Goodhart's
standing co-signing the Georgist tax-shift case in a formal macro model. Cited in
[Doucet's *Land is a Big Deal*](/wiki/land-is-a-big-deal/) (Chs. 15–16); this stub
de-references that citation pending a full read. [VERIFY: headline output-gain
