## Summary

"Welfare Effects of Property Taxation" by **Max Löffler** and **Sebastian
Siegloch** (IZA Discussion Paper No. 14195, 2021; also CESifo WP 8952, CEPR DP
15927, ZEW DP 21-026; an earlier version circulated as "Property Taxation, Local
Labor Markets and Rental Housing," 2015) is the leading modern quasi-experimental
study of recurrent property-tax incidence. Exploiting roughly **5,200 municipal
reforms** of Germany's Grundsteuer, the authors find that **tax increases are fully
passed on to rental prices within about three years** — and that **pass-through is
lower where housing supply is inelastic**.[1]

This is the long-flagged HONEST COUNTER-EVIDENCE page for
[landlords cannot pass LVT to tenants](/wiki/landlords-cannot-pass-lvt-to-tenants/)
(wired as `challenged_by`), and the wiki reports it at full strength: in a large,
well-identified modern setting, a recurrent property tax reached tenants
completely.

## Why It Challenges — and What It Leaves Standing

- **What it challenges:** any casual claim that "property taxes are never passed
  to tenants." German institutional conditions (its rental market structure, the
  Grundsteuer's base) produced full pass-through.
- **What it leaves standing:** the land-component argument. The German Grundsteuer
  falls on developed property including structures; the authors' own heterogeneity
  result — pass-through falls as housing supply becomes more inelastic[1] — points
  in exactly the direction the classical theory
  ([Mieszkowski](/wiki/mieszkowski-property-tax-incidence/);
  [Zodrow](/wiki/zodrow-three-views/)) predicts for a **pure land tax**, whose base
  is perfectly inelastic. The study does not test a land-only tax; the Danish
  land-tax evidence ([DØRS 2018](/wiki/dors-land-taxes-housing-prices/)) is the
  closest direct test and finds full capitalization into prices instead.
- US quasi-experimental evidence points the other way in a different institutional
  setting: [Schwegman & Yinger](/wiki/schwegman-yinger-homestead/) find only ~14%
  shifting in New York State.
