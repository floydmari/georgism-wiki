## Summary

In 2021, [Lars Doucet](/wiki/lars-doucet/) — a Norwegian-Texan game designer and self-described "pretty serious Georgist" — won **first place** in Scott Alexander's *Astral Codex Ten* Book Review Contest with a review of Henry George's *[Progress and Poverty](/wiki/progress-and-poverty/)*. The review was published on ACX (anonymously, as a contest finalist) on **16 April 2021**; Alexander announced the results, with Doucet's entry taking **first place**, on **10 July 2021**. It is widely credited as the spark of the **modern Georgism revival** online.

## Why It Matters

Doucet's review reintroduced George's 142-year-old argument to a large, analytically demanding audience — rationalists, effective altruists, and tech readers — who had never taken land seriously as an economic factor. The win was the launch point of a whole series: Alexander invited Doucet back as a guest author, and the resulting *[Does Georgism Work?](/wiki/doucet-does-georgism-work/)* essays began running on ACX on **9 December 2021**. The same body of work fed into his book *[Land Is a Big Deal](/wiki/land-is-a-big-deal/)* and his [Dwarkesh Podcast appearance](/wiki/dwarkesh-doucet-podcast/), and seeded interest among figures like [Vitalik Buterin](/wiki/buterin-on-radical-markets/) and Noah Smith. It is the single most influential piece of recent Georgist popular writing.
