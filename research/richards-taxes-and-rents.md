## Summary

This 2019 paper challenges the standard view that taxation only redistributes income *after* it is earned. It argues that tax policy actively shapes the **pre-tax** distribution by determining how much **rent extraction** is profitable in the first place.

## Key Argument

When tax systems leave economic rents — including [land rent](/wiki/economic-rent/) — lightly taxed, they make rent-seeking more lucrative than productive activity, drawing talent and capital toward extraction. Taxing rents (as with a [land value tax](/wiki/land-value-tax/)) reduces the payoff to [rent-seeking](/wiki/rent-seeking/) and shifts the pre-tax distribution toward earned income. Tax policy is thus a cause of inequality, not just a response to it.
