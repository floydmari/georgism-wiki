## Summary

Released **9 January 2023** — "**Lars Doucet — Progress, poverty, Georgism, & why rent is too damn high**" (the show was then still called *The Lunar Society*) — this roughly **1h40m** episode has host Dwarkesh Patel interview [Lars Doucet](/wiki/lars-doucet/), author of *[Land Is a Big Deal](/wiki/land-is-a-big-deal/)*, about Georgism, land value taxation, and assessment. Patel calls it "one of my best episodes ever," and it is frequently recommended as the best **single-sitting introduction** to the modern Georgist case.

## Why It Matters

The conversation covers the whole arc — why land is economically distinct, whether values are large enough to matter, the [incidence](/wiki/landlords-cannot-pass-lvt-to-tenants/) and [assessment](/wiki/land-cannot-be-assessed/) objections, and practical implementation. Patel said it "completely changed how I think about who creates value in the world and who leeches off it." As accessible audio, it has been a major on-ramp for newcomers. The episode draws directly on the research Doucet began after his prize-winning [ACX book review of *Progress and Poverty*](/wiki/doucet-acx-book-review/) and his [*Does Georgism Work?*](/wiki/doucet-does-georgism-work/) series.
