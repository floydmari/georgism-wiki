## Summary

*Real Estate 4 Ransom: Why Does Land Cost the Earth?* (2012, approximately 40
minutes) is a documentary produced by [Prosper Australia](/wiki/prosper-australia/)
and directed by [Karl Fitzgerald](/wiki/karl-fitzgerald/) and Gavin Emmanuel
(credits also render the surname "Emanuele" — spelling variant flagged), shot over
five years.[1] Its argument: the global financial crisis was substantially a
**land-speculation crisis** rather than a bank-lending story alone, and shifting
taxation off labor and enterprise onto land would aid entrepreneurs and home
ownership. [Michael Hudson](/wiki/michael-hudson/) — among the film's
interviewees — promoted it on its March 2012 release.[2]

It is the Georgist movement's most widely circulated film, freely viewable via
Prosper Australia and documentary platforms, and the wiki treats it as the video
counterpart of the [cycle narrative](/wiki/land-speculation-causes-cycles/):
useful for **deployment** (a 40-minute shareable statement of the case), never as
evidence — every factual claim it dramatizes is cited on the wiki from the
underlying literature ([18-year land cycle](/wiki/18-year-land-cycle/);
[speculation outcome](/wiki/lvt-dampens-land-speculation/);
[2008 financial crisis](/wiki/2008-financial-crisis/)).

## Provenance and Coverage

Martin Adams introduced the film to progress.org readers;[3] Prosper Australia
hosts the film and production notes.[1] Per the wiki's source hierarchy the film
and its coverage are level-6 advocacy sources — cited to represent the movement's
own position and outreach history.
