## Summary

This 1977 essay by [Joseph Stiglitz](/wiki/joseph-stiglitz/) is where the term **"Henry George Theorem"** was coined. It is the foundational theoretical statement that a single tax on land rent can finance public goods.

## The Result

Stiglitz showed that, for an optimally sized community providing public goods at the optimal level, the **aggregate land rent generated equals the optimal expenditure on those public goods**. Public goods raise the desirability of locations, which is capitalised into land rent (see [tax capitalization](/wiki/tax-capitalization/)); capturing that rent recovers exactly what the public spending created. He demonstrated this in a formal general-equilibrium setting, giving Henry George's 19th-century intuition a rigorous modern foundation.

## Relationship to Other Work

The result was elaborated the same era in [Arnott & Stiglitz (1979)](/wiki/arnott-stiglitz-henry-george-theorem/) and later extended to realistic "second-best" conditions by [Behrens et al. (2015)](/wiki/behrens-hgt-second-best/). Together these form the theoretical backbone of the [Henry George Theorem](/wiki/henry-george-theorem/).
